# UAE CIVIL CODE
# FEDERAL LAW NO. 5 OF 1985

## PART 1: GENERAL PRINCIPLES

### Article 1: Sources of Law
1. The Code.
2. Islamic Sharia (Maliki/Hanbali schools).
3. Custom (not conflicting with public order).

### Article 2: Interpretation
Rules of Islamic jurisprudence (*fiqh*) apply to interpretation.

### Article 106: Abuse of Rights
Liability for unlawful exercise of a right if:
- Intent to harm.
- Benefit is disproportionate to harm.
- Purpose is unlawful.

---

## PART 2: CONTRACT LAW

### Article 129: Essential Elements
1. Mutual consent.
2. Lawful subject matter.
3. Lawful cause (*causa*).

### Article 246: Good Faith
Contracts must be performed in accordance with their contents and in a manner consistent with the requirements of **good faith**.

### Article 249: Unforeseen Circumstances
Court may adjust "oppressive" obligations due to exceptional, unpredictable circumstances.

### Article 390: Liquidated Damages
Judge may increase or decrease fixed compensation to match the **actual loss** suffered.

---

## PART 3: CIVIL LIABILITY (TORT)

### Article 282: General Rule
Any harm done to another shall render the actor liable to make good the harm.

### Article 292: Assessment of Damages
Covers loss suffered and profit lost as a natural result of the harm.

### Article 318: Unjust Enrichment
Taking property without lawful cause requires return or compensation.

---

## PART 4: PROPERTY

### Article 1133: Right of Ownership
Absolute right to dispose of property within the limits of the law.

---

## PART 5: LIMITATION PERIODS

### Article 473: General Limitation
General civil claims: **15 years**.

### Article 298: Tort Claims
Claims for compensation: **3 years** from awareness of harm and perpetrator.
