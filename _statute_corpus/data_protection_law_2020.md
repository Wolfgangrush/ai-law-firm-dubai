# DIFC DATA PROTECTION LAW
# DIFC LAW NO. 5 OF 2020
*(Aligned with GDPR)*

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title
Cited as the "Data Protection Law 2020".

### Article 3: Application
Applies to the processing of personal data by a Controller or Processor in the DIFC.

---

## PART 2: DATA PROTECTION PRINCIPLES

### Article 9: Principles
Personal data must be:
- Processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently.
- Collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes.
- Adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary (Data Minimisation).
- Accurate and kept up to date.
- Kept in a form that permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary.
- Processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security.

---

## PART 3: DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

### Article 32: Withdrawal of Consent
Data subjects have the right to withdraw consent at any time.

### Article 33: Right of Access
The right to obtain confirmation and a copy of the personal data being processed.

### Article 35: Right to Erasure
The right to have personal data deleted (Right to be Forgotten) under certain conditions.

### Article 37: Data Portability
The right to receive data in a structured, machine-readable format.

### Article 38: Right to Object
The right to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.

### Article 39: Non-Discrimination
Prohibits discriminating against a data subject for exercising their rights.

---

## PART 4: CONTROLLER AND PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

### Article 14: Accountability
The Controller is responsible for, and must be able to demonstrate compliance with, the Law.

### Article 15: Data Protection by Design and Default
Privacy must be integrated into systems and services from the outset.

### Article 16: Data Protection Officer (DPO)
Mandatory appointment for public authorities and entities performing "High Risk Processing Activities".

### Article 20: Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)
Mandatory for processing likely to result in a high risk to individuals' rights.

### Article 24: Relationships with Processors
Must be governed by a legally binding written contract with specific security and compliance requirements.

---

## PART 7: ENFORCEMENT

### Article 41 & 42: Breach Notification
- **To Commissioner**: As soon as practicable if the breach compromises security or privacy.
- **To Data Subject**: Required if the breach results in a "high risk" to their rights.

### Article 61: Administrative Fines
Fines up to **$100,000** for standard contraventions.

### Article 64: Compensation
Data subjects may apply to the DIFC Courts for compensation for damage (including distress).
