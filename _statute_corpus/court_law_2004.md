# DIFC COURT LAW
# DIFC LAW NO. 10 OF 2004 / DUBAI LAW NO. 2 OF 2025

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title
Cited as the "DIFC Court Law 2004".
*Note: Consolidated under Dubai Law No. 2 of 2025.*

### Article 7: Establishment
The DIFC Courts are an independent judicial system within Dubai.

---

## PART 3: JURISDICTION

### Article 14 (Dubai Law No. 2 of 2025) / Article 5 (Law No. 12 of 2004)
The Courts have exclusive jurisdiction over:
- Civil or commercial claims where the DIFC or any DIFC Body is a party.
- Civil or commercial claims arising out of or relating to a contract or transaction performed in the DIFC.
- Civil or commercial claims arising out of or relating to any incident that occurred in the DIFC.
- Any claim over which the Courts have jurisdiction under DIFC Laws.

---

## PART 4: APPLICABLE LAW

### Article 30: Law to be Applied
(1) The Court must apply:
(a) DIFC Law; or
(b) Law chosen by the parties.
(2) The Court may consider the law of any other jurisdiction (e.g., English Common Law) if DIFC law is silent.

---

## PART 5: POWERS OF THE COURT

### Article 32: Injunctions and Interim Orders
The Court has broad powers to issue:
- Mandatory and prohibitory injunctions.
- Freezing orders (Mareva injunctions).
- Search orders (Anton Piller orders).

### Article 33: Contempt of Court
The Court has statutory power to punish for contempt.

---

## PART 7: ENFORCEMENT

### Article 42: Execution of Judgments
Sets out the framework for enforcing DIFC judgments outside the DIFC, particularly in onshore Dubai.

---

## PART 8: COSTS

### Article 38 (RDC): General Rule
The unsuccessful party pays the costs of the successful party (Loser Pays principle).
