# RULES OF THE DIFC COURTS (RDC)
# PROCEDURAL CODE

## PART 1: OVERRIDING OBJECTIVE
The overriding objective of these Rules is to enable the Court to deal with cases justly.

---

## PART 7 & 8: STARTING PROCEEDINGS
- **Part 7:** Used for claims involving substantial disputes of fact.
- **Part 8:** Used for claims involving interpretation of law or where facts are not in dispute.

---

## PART 9 & 10: SERVICE
- **Service:** Strict rules on how documents must be delivered to the other party.
- **Electronic Service:** Permitted with Court approval.

---

## PART 12: DISPUTING JURISDICTION
A defendant must file an Acknowledgment of Service and then a formal application if they wish to dispute the Court's jurisdiction.

---

## PART 24: SUMMARY JUDGMENT
The Court may give summary judgment if a party has "no real prospect" of succeeding or defending.

---

## PART 28: DISCLOSURE
Parties must disclose documents on which they rely and those which adversely affect their own case or support another party's case.

---

## PART 29: EVIDENCE
Evidence is typically provided via written **Witness Statements**, which serve as evidence-in-chief.

---

## PART 38: COSTS
General Rule: The unsuccessful party pays the costs of the successful party. Proportionality is key.

---

## PART 53: SMALL CLAIMS TRIBUNAL (SCT)
- **Jurisdiction:** Claims under AED 500,000 (unless parties agree to higher).
- **Representation:** Lawyers generally not permitted unless by exception.
