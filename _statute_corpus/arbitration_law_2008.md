# DIFC ARBITRATION LAW
# DIFC LAW NO. 1 OF 2008
*(Based on UNCITRAL Model Law)*

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title
Cited as the "Arbitration Law 2008".

### Article 3: Application
Applies to arbitrations where the **Seat of Arbitration** is the DIFC.

### Article 9: Extent of Court Intervention
In matters governed by this Law, no Court shall intervene except where so provided in this Law.

---

## PART 3: ARBITRATION

### Article 12: Definition and form of Arbitration Agreement
An arbitration agreement must be in writing.

### Article 16: Number of Arbitrators
The parties are free to determine the number of arbitrators. If they fail to agree, the number shall be three (3).

### Article 23: Competence of Arbitral Tribunal to rule on its jurisdiction
The "Competence-Competence" principle: The tribunal may rule on its own jurisdiction, including any objections with respect to the existence or validity of the arbitration agreement.

### Article 26: Equal treatment of parties
The parties shall be treated with equality and each party shall be given a full opportunity of presenting his case.

### Article 33: Experts appointed by Arbitral Tribunal
Unless otherwise agreed by the parties, the tribunal may appoint one or more experts to report to it on specific issues.

### Article 38: Rules applicable to substance of dispute
The tribunal shall decide the dispute in accordance with such rules of law as are chosen by the parties.

### Article 41: Application for setting aside as exclusive recourse against arbitral award
An arbitral award may be set aside by the DIFC Court only on limited grounds (e.g., incapacity, invalid agreement, lack of notice, award outside scope, procedural irregularity, public policy).

---

## PART 4: RECOGNITION AND ENFORCEMENT OF AWARDS

### Article 42: Recognition and enforcement
An arbitral award, irrespective of the State or jurisdiction in which it was made, shall be recognized as binding and enforced.

### Article 44: Grounds for refusing recognition or enforcement
Grounds mirror those for setting aside under Article 41, plus the ground that the award has not yet become binding or has been set aside in the country of origin.
