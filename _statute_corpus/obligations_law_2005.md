# DIFC LAW OF OBLIGATIONS
# DIFC LAW NO. 5 OF 2005

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title
Cited as the "Law of Obligations 2005".

### Article 9: Limitation Periods
(1) Generally 15 years from the date the cause of action arose.
(2) Shorter periods may apply to specific claims (e.g., 3 years for contribution claims).

### Article 10: Causation
A claimant must show that the loss would not have occurred "but for" the defendant's conduct and that the conduct was a "substantial cause".

---

## PART 2: CAPACITY

### Article 12: Capacity
Liability in tort or other obligations starts at age seven (7).

---

## PART 3: OBLIGATIONS OF GENERAL APPLICATION

### Article 17: Negligence
A person owes a duty of care if they should reasonably foresee that their act or omission might cause loss to another.

### Article 18: Standard of Care
The standard of a reasonable person in the same circumstances.

---

## PART 5: OBLIGATIONS RELATING TO BAILMENT

### Article 65: Bailment defined
Where a person (the Bailee) is in possession of property belonging to another (the Bailor).

### Article 67: Duty of Bailee
The Bailee must take reasonable care of the property.

---

## PART 8: FIDUCIARY DUTIES

### Article 158: Fiduciary defined
A person who has undertaken to act for another in circumstances giving rise to a relationship of trust and confidence.

### Article 159: Presumed Fiduciaries
The following are presumed fiduciaries:
- Directors (to the Company).
- Employees (to the Employer).
- Partners (to each other).
- Agents (to the Principal).

### Article 160: Duties of Fiduciaries
- Duty of loyalty.
- Duty to avoid conflicts of interest.
- Duty not to profit from the position without consent.
