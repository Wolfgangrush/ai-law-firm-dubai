# JURISDICTIONAL CONFLICTS (DUBAI VS. DIFC)
# DECREE NO. 19 OF 2016 / DECREE NO. 29 OF 2024

## OVERVIEW
Established the **Judicial Committee** (Joint Judicial Committee - JJC) to resolve jurisdictional conflicts between Dubai Courts (Mainland) and DIFC Courts (Offshore).

---

## CORE FUNCTIONS

### 1. Determining Jurisdiction
Deciding which court system has the legal authority to hear a specific claim when both claim jurisdiction.

### 2. Resolving Conflicting Judgments
Determining which judgment should be enforced if both court systems issue contradictory rulings on the same matter between the same parties.

---

## KEY PROVISIONS (DECREE 29 OF 2024)

### 1. Repeal of Decree 19
Decree No. 29 of 2024 repealed and replaced the 2016 Decree, renaming the body to the **"Judicial Authority for Resolving Jurisdictional Conflicts"**.

### 2. Finality of Decisions
Decisions made by the Authority are final, binding, and non-appealable. They carry the weight of legal precedent.

### 3. Tie-Breaking
The Authority consists of members from both Dubai and DIFC Courts. In the event of a tie, the Chairman (Head of the Dubai Court of Cassation) has the casting vote.

---

## PRACTICAL IMPLICATION FOR ADVOCATES
- **Stay of Proceedings:** Filing an application with the Authority typically stays (pauses) the underlying proceedings in both courts.
- **Conduit Jurisdiction:** The Authority has significantly limited the use of DIFC Courts as a "conduit" to enforce awards in Mainland without Mainland court oversight.
