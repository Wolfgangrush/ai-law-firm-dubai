# DIFC PERSONAL PROPERTY LAW
# DIFC LAW NO. 9 OF 2005

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title
Cited as the "Personal Property Law 2005".

### Article 3: Application
Applies within the jurisdiction of the DIFC.

---

## PART 3: TRANSFERS OF PROPERTY

### Article 8: Methods of Transfer
Property can be transferred by delivery, deed, or other legal instruments.

### Article 10: Good Faith Acquisition
A buyer acquires valid title if they act in good faith and rely on the owner's conduct to believe the seller has authority, even if authority was lacking.

### Article 11: Transfer of Future Property
Allows for the legal transfer of property that does not yet exist or is not yet owned by the transferor.

---

## PART 4: RIGHTS OF PARTIES

### Article 16: Warranties
Implied warranties include:
- Transferor has the right to sell.
- Property is free from undisclosed encumbrances.

### Article 18: Negotiable Instruments
Governs transfer of rights via checks or promissory notes.

---

## PART 5-7: INVESTMENTS

### Article 19: Certificated vs. Uncertificated
Distinguishes between physical and digital/book-entry investments. Rules for establishing "control" over assets.

---

## PART 8: INVESTMENT ENTITLEMENTS

### Article 25: Rights against Intermediaries
Defines the rights of an "entitlement holder" to assets held by an intermediary or custodian.

---

## RELATION TO OTHER LAWS
- **Digital Assets Law 2024:** Specifically addresses cryptocurrencies and NFTs.
- **Law of Security 2024:** Governs use of personal property as collateral.
