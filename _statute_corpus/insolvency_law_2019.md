# DIFC INSOLVENCY LAW
# DIFC LAW NO. 1 OF 2019

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title
Cited as the "Insolvency Law 2019".

### Article 3: Application
Applies to companies registered in the DIFC.

---

## PART 3: REHABILITATION

### Article 14: Eligibility for Rehabilitation
A company is eligible if it is or is likely to become unable to pay its debts.

### Article 15: Automatic Moratorium
Upon filing for rehabilitation, an automatic **120-day moratorium** is granted, preventing creditors from taking enforcement actions.

### Article 16: Debtor-in-Possession
Directors typically remain in control of the company during the rehabilitation process, unless an administrator is appointed.

---

## PART 4: ADMINISTRATION

### Article 32: Appointment of Administrator
The Court may appoint an administrator if there is evidence of mismanagement or misconduct.

---

## PART 6: WINDING UP

### Article 50: Modes of Winding Up
(1) Voluntary Winding Up (by shareholders).
(2) Compulsory Winding Up (by court order).

### Article 62: Grounds for Compulsory Winding Up
Includes the company being unable to pay its debts.

---

## PART 7: CROSS-BORDER INSOLVENCY
*(Incorporates UNCITRAL Model Law)*

### Article 100: Purpose
To provide effective mechanisms for dealing with cases of cross-border insolvency and promoting cooperation between DIFC and foreign courts.

### Article 105: Recognition of Foreign Proceedings
Provides for the recognition of foreign main and non-main proceedings.
