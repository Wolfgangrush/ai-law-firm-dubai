# DIFC REGULATORY LAW
# DIFC LAW NO. 1 OF 2004

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title
Cited as the "Regulatory Law 2004".

### Article 2: Legislative Authority
This Law is made by the Ruler of Dubai.

---

## PART 2: THE DUBAI FINANCIAL SERVICES AUTHORITY (DFSA)

### Article 7: Establishment of the DFSA
Establishes the DFSA as an independent body with its own legal personality.

### Article 8: Objectives of the DFSA
(a) To foster and maintain financial stability in the DIFC.
(b) To prevent, detect, and restrain conduct that may damage the reputation of the DIFC.
(c) To protect users of financial services in the DIFC.

### Article 23: Power to make Rules
The DFSA Board may make Rules (the DFSA Rulebook) for the purposes of the Law.

---

## PART 3: THE FINANCIAL SERVICES PROHIBITION

### Article 41: General Prohibition
No person shall carry on a "Financial Service" in or from the DIFC unless they are an **Authorised Firm** or an **Authorised Market Institution**.

### Article 42: Financial Services
Definitions of "Financial Services" are set out in the Rules.

---

## PART 5: SUPERVISION AND ENFORCEMENT

### Article 62: Power to obtain information
The DFSA may require any person to provide information or produce documents.

### Article 76: Enforcement
The DFSA has the power to:
- Impose financial penalties (fines).
- Issue public censures.
- Suspend or withdraw licenses/authorizations.
- Prohibit individuals from performing functions in the DIFC.

---

## PART 6: THE FINANCIAL MARKETS TRIBUNAL (FMT)

### Article 83: Establishment of the FMT
Establishes the FMT as an independent appeals body to review decisions made by the DFSA.
