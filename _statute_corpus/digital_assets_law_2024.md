# DIFC DIGITAL ASSETS LAW
# DIFC LAW NO. 2 OF 2024

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title
This Law may be cited as the "Digital Assets Law 2024".

### Article 2: Legislative Authority
This Law is made by the Ruler of Dubai.

### Article 3: Application of the Law
(1) This Law applies in the jurisdiction of the DIFC.
(2) This Law applies to Digital Assets as defined herein.

### Article 4: Date of Enactment
This Law is enacted on the date specified in the Enactment Notice.

### Article 5: Commencement
This Law comes into force on the date specified in the Enactment Notice.

### Article 6: Interpretation
The Schedule to this Law contains:
(a) interpretative provisions which apply to this Law; and
(b) a list of defined terms used in this Law.

---

## PART 2: DIGITAL ASSETS

### Article 7: Nature of Digital Assets
(1) A Digital Asset is a thing in action.
(2) A Digital Asset is not a thing in possession.

### Article 8: Legal Recognition of Digital Assets
A Digital Asset shall not be denied legal effect, validity, or enforceability solely on the grounds that it is in digital form.

---

## PART 3: CONTROL OF DIGITAL ASSETS

### Article 9: Control
(1) A person has "Control" of a Digital Asset if:
(a) the Digital Asset, or the system in which it exists, confers on that person:
(i) the power to enjoy substantially all the benefit of the Digital Asset;
(ii) the power to prevent others from enjoying substantially all the benefit of the Digital Asset; and
(iii) the power to transfer those powers to another person; and
(b) the Digital Asset, or the system in which it exists, allows the person to identify themselves as having the powers set out in Article 9(1)(a).

### Article 10: Change of Control
A change of Control occurs when a person acquires the powers set out in Article 9(1)(a), whether or not there is a transfer of the Digital Asset itself.

### Article 11: Effect of Control
Control of a Digital Asset is equivalent to possession of a tangible moveable property for the purposes of applying other DIFC Laws, unless the context otherwise requires.

### Article 12: Control by Multiple Persons
Two or more persons may have Control of a Digital Asset jointly or concurrently.

---

## PART 4: TITLE AND TRANSFER

### Article 13: Title to Digital Assets
(1) Legal title to a Digital Asset is held by the person who has Control of it.
(2) Equitable title to a Digital Asset may be held by a person other than the person who has Control.

### Article 14: Transfer of Digital Assets
(1) A Digital Asset is transferred when a person (the "Transferor") takes steps to cause another person (the "Transferee") to obtain Control of the Digital Asset.
(2) A transfer is effective to pass legal title if the Transferee obtains Control.

### Article 15: Innocent Acquisition
(1) A person who acquires Control of a Digital Asset for value and without notice of any adverse claim (an "Innocent Acquisition") takes the Digital Asset free of any adverse claim.
(2) An "adverse claim" means a claim that a person has a proprietary interest in the Digital Asset and that it is a violation of the rights of that person for another person to hold, transfer, or otherwise deal with the Digital Asset.

---

## PART 5: MISCELLANEOUS

### Article 16: Governing Law
The governing law of a Digital Asset shall be determined in accordance with the Law of Obligations and other applicable DIFC Laws.

### Article 17: Jurisdiction
The DIFC Courts have jurisdiction over disputes arising under this Law.

### Article 18: Power to make Regulations
The Board of Directors of the DIFC Authority may make Regulations for the purposes of this Law.

---

## SCHEDULE 1: INTERPRETATION

### 1. Rules of Interpretation
(1) In this Law, a reference to:
(a) a statutory provision includes a reference to that provision as amended;
(b) a "person" includes any natural person, body corporate or body unincorporate, partnership, government, state, or state agency.

### 2. Defined Terms
*   **Digital Asset**: A digital representation of value or rights which can be transferred and stored electronically, using distributed ledger technology or similar technology.
*   **Control**: As defined in Article 9.
*   **DIFC**: The Dubai International Financial Centre.
*   **Thing in Action**: A right that can be enforced by legal action (intangible property).
