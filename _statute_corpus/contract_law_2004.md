# DIFC CONTRACT LAW
# DIFC LAW NO. 6 OF 2004

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title and repeal
Cited as the "Contract Law 2004".

### Article 3: Application
Applies to contracts and agreements made within the jurisdiction of the DIFC.

---

## PART 3: FORMATION OF CONTRACT

### Article 14: Manner of Formation
A contract is concluded by the acceptance of an offer. No writing requirement unless agreed or required by law.

### Article 15: Definition of Offer
A proposal is an offer if it is sufficiently definite and indicates the intention of the offeror to be bound.

### Article 23: Mode of Acceptance
Conduct indicating assent is acceptance. Silence or inactivity is not acceptance.

### Article 29: Duty of Confidentiality
Parties must not disclose confidential information exchanged during negotiations, even if no contract is concluded.

---

## PART 5: VALIDITY

### Article 37: Mistake
A party may avoid the contract for mistake if the mistake was reasonable and the other party knew or ought to have known of it.

### Article 40: Fraud
A party may avoid the contract when it has been led to conclude the contract by the other party's fraudulent representation.

### Article 43: Gross Disparity
A party may avoid the contract if, at the time of the conclusion of the contract, the contract gave the other party an excessive advantage (Gross Disparity).

---

## PART 8: NON-PERFORMANCE (BREACH)

### Article 77: Non-performance defined
Failure by a party to perform any obligation, including defective or late performance.

### Article 82: Force Majeure
Non-performance is excused if due to an impediment beyond the party's control that could not reasonably have been anticipated.

### Article 86: Termination for Fundamental Breach
A party may terminate where the failure of the other party to perform an obligation amounts to a **fundamental non-performance**.

---

## PART 11: DAMAGES AND REMEDIES

### Article 109: Right to Damages
Any non-performance gives a right to damages unless excused.

### Article 110: Full Compensation
Entitled to full compensation, including loss suffered and gain deprived (loss of profit).

### Article 122: Liquidated Damages
Entitled to the sum specified in the contract regardless of actual harm. The Court may reduce it if it is **grossly excessive**.
