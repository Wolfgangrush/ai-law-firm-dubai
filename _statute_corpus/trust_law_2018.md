# DIFC TRUST LAW
# DIFC LAW NO. 4 OF 2018

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title
Cited as the "Trust Law 2018".

### Article 3 & 4: Application
Applies to DIFC trusts and foreign trusts acting within the DIFC.

### Article 9: Mandatory Rules
Terms of the trust prevail except for mandatory rules, including:
- Requirement for a trust to be for the benefit of beneficiaries or a lawful purpose.
- Trustee’s duty to act in good faith.
- Power of the Court to modify or terminate a trust.

---

## PART 2: GOVERNING LAW

### Article 11: Choice of Law
Settlors can expressly choose DIFC law. If no law is specified, the law with the "closest connection" applies.

### Article 14: Foreign Law (Firewall)
Protects DIFC trusts from foreign "forced heirship" claims or matrimonial orders. Validity is determined by DIFC law.

---

## PART 4: CREATION AND VALIDITY

### Article 35: Requirements for Creation
- Settlor must have capacity.
- Intention to create a trust.
- Definite beneficiary or lawful purpose.

### Article 42: Duration
DIFC trusts can have **perpetual existence** (no limit on duration).

---

## PART 8: DUTIES AND POWERS OF TRUSTEES

### Article 56: General Duties
Trustees must administer the trust in good faith, in accordance with its terms, and solely in the interests of the beneficiaries.

### Article 58: Impartiality
Trustees must act impartially between two or more beneficiaries.

### Article 59: Prudent Administration
Trustees must exercise reasonable care, skill, and caution.

---

## PART 10: RESERVED POWERS AND PROTECTORS

### Article 82: Settlor’s Reserved Powers
Settlors may reserve powers without invalidating the trust, including:
- Power to revoke or amend.
- Power to appoint/remove trustees or beneficiaries.
- Power to direct investments.

### Article 83: Protectors
Recognizes the role of a "Protector" who can oversee the trustee.
