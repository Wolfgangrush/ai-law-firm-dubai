# DIFC COMPANIES LAW
# DIFC LAW NO. 5 OF 2018

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title and repeal
(1) Cited as the "Companies Law 2018".
(2) Repeals and replaces the Companies Law 2009.

### Article 3: Application of this Law
Applies within the jurisdiction of the DIFC to any person conducting business in or from the DIFC.

---

## PART 3: INCORPORATION AND REGISTRATION

### Article 8: Types of companies
(1) **Private Company**: Minimum one (1) Shareholder.
(2) **Public Company**: Minimum one (1) Shareholder, but may offer shares to the public.
(3) **Recognised Company**: Foreign company registered in the DIFC.

### Article 9: Legal personality
A Company has a separate legal personality. Shareholders' liability is limited to the unpaid amount on their shares.

### Article 11: Articles of Association
Must be in English. Companies may adopt **Standard Articles** or provide their own.

### Article 16: Alteration of Articles
Amended only by **Special Resolution** (75% majority).

---

## PART 5: CORPORATE CAPACITY AND TRANSACTIONS

### Article 20 & 21: Capacity
A Company has the capacity of a natural person. Acts are not invalid solely due to lack of capacity in the Articles.

### Article 24: Execution of Documents
Signed by two Directors, or one Director and the Secretary, or one Director in the presence of a witness.

---

## PART 7: SHARES AND SHARE CAPITAL

### Article 31: Nature of Shares
Shares are personal property and transferable as per the Articles.

### Article 33: Pre-emption Rights
Existing shareholders have a right of first refusal on new equity securities (Proportionate to their holding). Private companies can opt out.

### Article 38: Payment for Shares
Paid in money or money's worth. Public companies must have 25% paid up on allotment.

---

## PART 9: DIRECTORS AND MANAGEMENT

### Article 68: Number of Directors
- Private Company: Minimum one (1) Director.
- Public Company: Minimum two (2) Directors.

### Article 70-75: Statutory Duties of Directors
1. **Duty to act within powers**: Comply with Articles.
2. **Duty to promote the success of the Company**: Act in good faith for the benefit of members as a whole.
3. **Duty to exercise independent judgment**.
4. **Duty to exercise reasonable care, skill, and diligence**.
5. **Duty to avoid conflicts of interest**.
6. **Duty not to accept benefits from third parties**.
7. **Duty to declare interest in transactions**.

### Article 92: Annual General Meetings
- Public Companies: Mandatory within 6 months of year-end.
- Private Companies: Optional unless required by Articles.

### Article 94: Resolutions
- **Ordinary Resolution**: Simple majority (>50%).
- **Special Resolution**: 75% majority.

---

## PART 12: ACCOUNTS AND AUDIT

### Article 122: Maintenance of Records
Companies must keep accounting records for at least six (6) years.

### Article 131: Audit Requirement
- **Small Private Companies**: (Turnover < $5M, <= 20 shareholders) are exempt from audit and filing accounts with the Registrar.
- Other companies must have accounts audited.
