# DIFC EMPLOYMENT LAW
# DIFC LAW NO. 2 OF 2019
*(Consolidated with Amendments up to 2024)*

## PART 1: GENERAL

### Article 1: Title and repeal
(1) This Law may be cited as the "Employment Law 2019".
(2) It repeals and replaces the Employment Law 2005.

### Article 2: Legislative Authority
This Law is made by the Ruler of Dubai.

### Article 3: Purpose of this Law
The purpose of this Law is to provide a legal framework for the regulation of employment in the DIFC, promote efficient operation, and balance the needs of Employers and Employees.

### Article 4: Application of the Law
(1) Applies to Employers with a place of business in the DIFC and Employees based in or ordinarily working in the DIFC.
(2) Does not apply to Secondees (except for specific Parts) or government entities.

### Article 7: Schedules
(1) Schedule 1 contains definitions and rules of interpretation.
(2) Schedule 2 contains contraventions and fines.

### Article 10: Limitation Period
Claims must be brought within six (6) months of the Termination Date.

---

## PART 2: HIRING EMPLOYEES

### Article 11: No waiver
The requirements of this Law are minimum standards. Waiver is void unless in a valid settlement agreement.

### Article 13: Hiring children
Employment of persons under sixteen (16) is prohibited.

### Article 14: Written employment contract
Employers must provide a written contract in English within seven (7) days of commencement. Must include wages, hours, leave, notice periods, and probation (max 6 months).

---

## PART 3: PROTECTION OF REMUNERATION

### Article 18: Paydays
Remuneration must be paid within seven (7) days after the end of the pay period.

### Article 19: Payments following termination
Outstanding wages and gratuity must be paid within fourteen (14) days of termination. Late payment may incur a penalty of a daily wage per day of delay.

---

## PART 4: WORKING TIME AND LEAVE

### Article 22: Maximum weekly working time
Average of forty-eight (48) hours per seven (7) days, unless the employee consents in writing to more.

### Article 23: Ramadan working hours
Fasting Muslim employees get a 2-hour reduction in daily working time without pay reduction.

### Article 27: Vacation Leave
Minimum of twenty (20) work days per year for employees with 90+ days of service.

### Article 31: Public Holidays
Entitled to leave on Public Holidays falling on a Work Day. Work on a holiday requires 100% pay or a substitute day.

### Article 33 & 34: Sick Leave and Pay
(1) Up to sixty (60) work days per year.
(2) Pay rates:
    - First 10 days: 100%
    - Next 20 days: 50%
    - Remaining 30 days: Unpaid.

---

## PART 5: PARENTAL LEAVE

### Article 37 & 38: Maternity Leave and Pay
(1) Up to sixty-five (65) work days.
(2) Pay: 100% for first 33 days, 50% for next 32 days (requires 12 months service).

### Article 39: Paternity Leave
Five (5) work days of paid leave (requires 12 months service).

---

## PART 9: NON-DISCRIMINATION

### Article 59: Discrimination
Prohibits discrimination on grounds of sex, marital status, race, nationality, religion, age, or disability.

---

## PART 10: TERMINATION AND GRATUITY (DEWS)

### Article 62 & 63: Termination for cause
Parties may terminate for cause (misconduct). Accrued gratuity/DEWS cannot be forfeited.

### Article 64: Notice Periods
- < 3 months service: 7 days
- 3 months to 5 years: 30 days
- > 5 years: 90 days

### Article 66: End of Service Benefits
(1) **Pre-Feb 2020:** Statutory Gratuity (21 days for first 5 years, 30 days thereafter) based on final basic wage.
(2) **Post-Feb 2020 (DEWS):** Mandatory monthly contributions to a Qualifying Scheme.
    - < 5 years service: 5.83% of Basic Wage.
    - > 5 years service: 8.33% of Basic Wage.
    - Basic Wage must be at least 50% of total remuneration.
