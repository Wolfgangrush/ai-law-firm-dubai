# 72-disclosure-production-of-documents-Mainland.md

## Source URL
https://elaws.moj.gov.ae/ (Federal Decree-Law No. 42 of 2022 on Civil Procedure)

## Last Verified
2026-05-28

## System
## System: Mainland

## Authority Tier
Primary Statute (Federal Decree-Law No. 42 of 2022, Arts 38-49 on Evidence + Civil Transactions Law)

## Language Variants
Arabic (Mandatory)

## Currency Warning
Mainland UAE civil procedure is inquisitorial — there is NO equivalent to DIFC common-law disclosure. Parties produce documents on which they rely (the "principle of best evidence"); court may ORDER production of specific documents on application. No standing duty of mutual disclosure as in DIFC / common-law systems.

## Template Type
Procedural Application / Court Order Request

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Heading** — Court of First Instance · Dubai Courts (or Federal Court) · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Petition for Order Compelling Production of Documents" (طلب أمر بإبراز مستندات).
3. **Identification of opposing party** — party in possession of documents.
4. **Specific documents sought** — identified with precision:
   - Document type (contract · invoice · bank statement · correspondence).
   - Date or date range.
   - Subject matter.
   - Why the document is decisive to the claim / defence.
5. **Relevance** — explicit statement of how the document relates to disputed facts.
6. **Possession** — evidence that opposing party has possession / custody / control.
7. **Necessity** — why the document cannot be obtained from any other source.
8. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Order compelling opposing party to produce specified documents within prescribed period.
   - Costs.
9. **Annexures** — pleadings · existing evidence · correspondence requesting production.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Arabic mandatory** — all court filings in Arabic; translations required for foreign-language documents (legalised translator).
- **Specificity** — generic requests refused; document-by-document specification required.
- **No "fishing expedition"** — Mainland court rejects broad categories.
- **No common-law privilege** — Mainland system protects only banking secrecy + state secrets + lawyer-client confidentiality (under Federal Law 23/1991 on Legal Profession).
- **Notarisation requirement** — foreign documents must be (1) attested in country of origin; (2) authenticated by UAE embassy abroad; (3) attested by UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs; (4) legally translated to Arabic.
- **Expert use** — court frequently appoints expert (Khabir) who may have access to broader documents.
- **Refusal consequences** — court may draw inferences against party refusing production.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The court may, on the application of a party, order the opposing party to produce specific documents in its possession that are relevant to the issues in dispute. The application must identify the documents with sufficient precision." (Federal Decree-Law No. 42 of 2022, Article 39, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Mainland production is narrow compared to common-law disclosure. Solo practitioners must (a) identify decisive documents pre-litigation; (b) request voluntarily before court application; (c) draft applications with surgical specificity. The court-appointed Khabir (expert) is often the practical route to broader document access — expert reports cite documents reviewed.
