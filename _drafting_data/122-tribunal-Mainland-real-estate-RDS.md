## Currency Warning
Dubai Real Estate disputes channelled through specialist tribunals: (a) **Rental Disputes Settlement Centre (RDSC)** — landlord-tenant disputes; (b) **Dubai Land Department Judicial Service** — title disputes · disputes related to off-plan sales; (c) **Dubai Courts Real Estate Department** — general real estate matters.

## Template Type
Tribunal Filing

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) RDSC CLAIM** (مركز فض المنازعات الإيجارية)

1. **Online filing** via DLD portal or in-person at RDSC.
2. **Applicant** — landlord or tenant.
3. **Respondent** — other party.
4. **Property details** — address · Ejari registration number · tenancy contract reference.
5. **Claim particulars**:
   - Type — eviction · rent dispute · maintenance · deposit return · contract renewal · rent increase challenge.
   - Sum claimed (if monetary).
6. **Ejari verification** — mandatory; tenancy contracts must be Ejari-registered.
7. **Conciliation** — first hearing typically attempts settlement.
8. **Tribunal hearing** — if conciliation fails; adjudicator-led; documentary + oral evidence.
9. **Judgment** — rapid; typically within 30 days of hearing.
10. **Appeal** — to Dubai Court of Appeal Real Estate Department on questions of law.

**(B) OFF-PLAN SALES DISPUTES**

1. **Dubai Land Department Judicial Service** handles disputes related to off-plan sales (Federal Law 8/2007 + Dubai amendments).
2. **Common claims** — developer delay · breach of payment terms · cancellation of contract · escrow account issues (Dubai Decree 27/2007 — escrow protection).
3. **Mandatory escrow** — developers must use escrow account for off-plan project payments.

**(C) TITLE + REGISTRATION DISPUTES**

1. **Dubai Land Department + Dubai Courts Real Estate Department** jurisdiction.
2. **Common claims** — forgery · improper registration · joint-ownership disputes · inheritance of real estate.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Arabic mandatory** for all real-estate-tribunal filings.
- **Ejari registration** — gateway requirement; unregistered tenancy contracts not enforceable.
- **Rent Increase Calculator** — Dubai Decree 43/2013 caps annual rent increases based on rental index.
- **Eviction notice** — Dubai Law 26/2007: 12 months' notice for eviction; specific grounds required.
- **Tenant rights** — security of tenure · proper notice · maintenance obligations on landlord.
- **Landlord rights** — eviction for non-payment · breach of contract · sale of property · personal use.
- **Real Estate Regulatory Agency (RERA)** — regulatory body under DLD.
- **Escrow protection** — Decree 27/2007 mandatory escrow for off-plan sales.
- **DIFC real estate** — separate DIFC Real Property Law; DIFC Courts jurisdiction.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"All disputes arising from rental relationships in the Emirate of Dubai shall be referred to the Rental Disputes Settlement Centre, which shall have exclusive jurisdiction to determine such disputes. Tenancy contracts must be registered with Ejari." (Dubai Decree No. 28 of 2013, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Real estate disputes are high-volume in Dubai given the size of the real estate market. Solo practitioners must triage: (a) tenancy → RDSC; (b) off-plan → DLD Judicial Service; (c) title → Dubai Courts Real Estate Department. RDSC procedures are tenant-friendly + rapid; landlords must follow strict notice procedures (12 months for eviction). Ejari registration is the principal procedural gateway.
