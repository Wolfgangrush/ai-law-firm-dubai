## System
## System: SHARED (DIFC + Mainland)

## Authority Tier
DIFC: DIFC Court Practice Directions + DIFC Bar Association rules · Mainland: Federal Law 23/1991 on Legal Profession (as amended)

## Language Variants
DIFC: English (Primary) · Mainland: Arabic (Mandatory)

## Currency Warning
DIFC permits foreign-qualified lawyers to act before DIFC Courts via Registration of Practitioners regime. Mainland UAE legal profession restricted to UAE-licensed advocates registered with Bar Association of relevant Emirate. Cross-engagement (DIFC counsel + Mainland advocate) common in dual-track matters.

## Template Type
Letter (Instructing Solicitor → Counsel / Advocate)

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) DIFC COUNSEL BRIEF**

1. Letterhead — instructing firm.
2. Date · Addressee — DIFC counsel chambers.
3. **Re** — DIFC Court · case number · parties.
4. **Confidentiality recital** — communication subject to legal advice + litigation privilege.
5. **Engagement scope**:
   - Trial advocacy.
   - Appellate advocacy (DIFC Court of Appeal).
   - Interlocutory application.
   - Pre-trial advisory.
   - Settlement negotiation.
6. **Conflict-check confirmation request**.
7. **Client identification** — name · authorised representative · capacity.
8. **Factual background** — chronological narrative (1-3 pages).
9. **Procedural posture**.
10. **Strategic objectives**.
11. **Materials enclosed** — schedule:
    - Pleadings (Claim Form · Particulars of Claim · Defence · Reply).
    - Witness Statements + AEICs.
    - Documents disclosed.
    - Prior counsel opinions.
    - Expert reports.
12. **Specific instructions**.
13. **Settlement authority** — client's instructions on settlement range.
14. **Fee arrangement** — hourly rate · brief fee + refresher · capped or fixed where applicable.
15. **Timeline**.
16. **Privileged communications** preserved.
17. **Closing**.
18. **Signature**.

**(B) MAINLAND DUBAI ADVOCATE BRIEF** (مذكرة تكليف محامي)

1. Letterhead of instructing firm.
2. **Heading** in Arabic + English.
3. **Engagement scope** — court appearances · pleading preparation · negotiations.
4. **UAE-licensed advocate** required — must be registered with relevant Emirate's Bar Association (Dubai · Abu Dhabi · etc.).
5. **Power of Attorney (POA)** — separate notarised POA in favour of advocate; mandatory for court appearances.
6. **Materials in Arabic** — pleadings · evidence · prior submissions.
7. **Specific instructions** — drafting statement of claim · defence · appeal · cassation · execution.
8. **Fee arrangement** — typically:
   - Fixed fee per stage (first instance · appeal · cassation · execution).
   - Success fee permitted under Federal Law 23/1991 (capped at 20% of award typically).
9. **Reporting cadence**.
10. **Translation of foreign documents** — by court-recognised translator.

## Drafting Conventions
- **DIFC vs Mainland advocate** — distinct profession; cross-briefing between is common.
- **DIFC POA** — DIFC Court accepts foreign POAs subject to authentication.
- **Mainland POA** — must be notarised in UAE OR legalised abroad + apostilled + authenticated by UAE Embassy + MoFA + Arabic translation.
- **Foreign-qualified DIFC counsel** — registered as "Registered DIFC Practitioner"; may appear at DIFC Court.
- **Success fees** — DIFC permits within professional-conduct limits; Mainland Federal Law 23/1991 caps at typically 20%.
- **Privileged communications** — DIFC follows English principles; Mainland privilege framework narrower (Federal Law 23/1991 Art 42).
- **Conflict of interest** — both regimes prohibit; verify before instruction.
- **Cross-jurisdictional matters** — DIFC counsel + Mainland advocate often co-engaged for dual-track matters.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"An advocate must maintain the confidentiality of communications received from the client and must promote and protect the client's interests within the limits of the law and professional ethics." (Federal Law 23/1991 on Legal Profession + DIFC Bar Association Rules, synthesised.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Dual-track Dubai matters typically require both DIFC counsel + Mainland advocate. Solo practitioners must triage: (a) DIFC matters → register foreign-qualified counsel OR brief DIFC-resident counsel; (b) Mainland matters → engage UAE-licensed advocate with notarised POA. Materials translation + apostille chain critical for cross-border POAs.
