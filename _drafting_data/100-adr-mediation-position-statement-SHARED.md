## System
## System: SHARED (DIFC + Mainland)

## Authority Tier
DIFC: RDC Part 27 + DIFC Mediation Rules · Mainland: Federal Law 17/2016 on Mediation (Dubai-specific) · International: Singapore Convention on Mediation

## Language Variants
DIFC mediation: English · Mainland mediation: Arabic (with English permitted by agreement) · DIAC: bilingual common

## Currency Warning
Dubai mediation landscape: (a) DIFC Court-encouraged mediation under RDC Part 27 (b) DIAC Mediation Centre — international hub; (c) Dubai Mainland mediation via Mediation Centre at Dubai Courts (Federal Law 17/2016) ; (d) Singapore Convention enforceability for cross-border mediated settlements (UAE acceded August 2019, in force March 2021).

## Template Type
Pleading / Position Paper (WITHOUT PREJUDICE — for mediator only)

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Header**:
   - Mediator's name + accreditation (DIFC-LCIA · DIAC · Dubai Court Mediation Centre · IMI-accredited · etc.).
   - Parties.
   - Date of mediation session.
   - Marking: **"WITHOUT PREJUDICE — FOR MEDIATOR'S EYES ONLY"**.
2. **Subject-Matter of the Dispute** — 2-3 paragraphs.
3. **Procedural Posture**:
   - Court proceedings on foot (DIFC Court · Dubai Courts · case number · stage) OR pre-action.
   - Court-ordered mediation OR contractual mediation OR voluntary.
4. **Client's Position on the Merits**:
   - Key facts.
   - Cause of action / defence.
   - Statutory + case-law basis (DIFC law / Mainland UAE law).
5. **Quantum**:
   - Claim value (principal · interest · costs).
   - Cost-of-litigation estimate.
6. **BATNA**:
   - Likely outcome at trial / award.
   - Time to judgment (12-24 months from current stage).
   - Enforcement risk (cross-border + DIFC-Dubai considerations).
7. **Settlement parameters**:
   - Aspirational outcome.
   - Reasonable outcome.
   - Walk-away threshold (CONFIDENTIAL TO MEDIATOR).
   - Non-monetary terms (apology · ongoing relationship · NDA · injunctive relief).
8. **Annexures** — key contracts · correspondence · expert valuations · prior settlement offers.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Two versions** — (a) shared with opposing party (filtered); (b) mediator-only (full BATNA).
- **WITHOUT PREJUDICE marking** mandatory.
- **Concise** — typically 8-15 pages.
- **Bilingual considerations** — if mediation conducted in Arabic, English version for international parties.
- **Confidentiality + privilege** — DIFC mediation protected under common-law principles; Mainland mediation protected under Federal Law 17/2016 Art 14; DIAC mediation under DIAC Mediation Rules.
- **Court-ordered mediation** — failure to engage in good faith risks adverse costs orders.
- **Singapore Convention** — for international mediated settlements where one party is from a different Convention State; enforces directly without re-litigation.
- **DIAC Mediation** — increasingly used for international commercial disputes seated in UAE.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Communications made in the course of mediation are confidential and shall not be admissible in any subsequent court or arbitration proceedings, subject to the exceptions provided by law." (DIFC RDC Part 27 + Federal Law 17/2016 + Singapore Convention, synthesised.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Dubai mediation is increasingly mandatory or contractually-required. Solo practitioners should treat mediation seriously — refusing to engage in good faith risks adverse costs orders. The two-version discipline (filtered for opposing party, full for mediator) is the key drafting craft. Singapore Convention enforceability is a significant advantage for cross-border commercial settlements seated in UAE.
