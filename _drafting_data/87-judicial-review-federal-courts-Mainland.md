## System
## System: Mainland

## Authority Tier
Primary Statute (Federal Law 17/1978 + Federal Decree-Law 42/2022) + UAE Constitution Article 25

## Language Variants
Arabic (Mandatory)

## Currency Warning
Mainland UAE administrative-law challenges proceed through Federal Court of First Instance (Administrative Division) + Federal Supreme Court for federal-government decisions. Emirate-government decisions challenged at relevant Emirate's Court (Dubai Court of First Instance). Time-limit: 60 days from notification of decision; extendable in limited circumstances.

## Template Type
Originating Application

## Structural Skeleton

**Petition for Annulment of Administrative Decision** (طعن في القرار الإداري)

1. **Heading** — Federal Court of First Instance · Administrative Division (or Dubai Court of First Instance for Emirate-level decisions) · case number · year.
2. **Title** — "Petition for Annulment of Administrative Decision dated [Date]".
3. **Applicant** — person aggrieved.
4. **Respondent** — government authority + Minister (for federal) OR Emirate department + Director (for Emirate).
5. **Decision impugned** — date · authority · operative terms.
6. **Time-limit** — 60 days from notification (verify current per Federal Law 17/1978).
7. **Standing** — applicant directly + personally affected.
8. **Grounds for annulment**:
   - **Lack of jurisdiction** (عدم الاختصاص) — decision-maker exceeded authority.
   - **Violation of form** (مخالفة الشكل) — procedural requirements not observed.
   - **Violation of law** (مخالفة القانون) — substantive law misapplied.
   - **Abuse of discretion** (إساءة استعمال السلطة) — bad faith · improper purpose.
   - **Lack of cause** (انعدام السبب) — no factual basis for decision.
   - **Misinterpretation of facts** (الخطأ في تكييف الوقائع).
9. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Annulment of decision.
   - Mandatory order compelling correct action.
   - Compensation for damages caused by unlawful decision.
   - Costs.
10. **Annexures** — decision · reasons (if obtained) · prior administrative appeals · evidence.

**Exhaustion of Administrative Remedies**

- Many decisions require internal administrative appeal first (تظلم إداري).
- Internal appeal typically within 30 days of decision.
- Failure to exhaust internal route may dismiss judicial challenge.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Arabic mandatory** — all court filings in Arabic.
- **60-day time-limit** strict; clock starts on notification (الإعلان).
- **Standing** — direct + personal interest; mere taxpayer / citizen interest insufficient.
- **Public-policy + Constitutional grounds** — UAE Constitution Art 25 + general principles.
- **Compensation claim** — may be coupled with annulment petition; quantified separately.
- **Stay of decision** pending hearing — separate urgent application; not automatic.
- **Federal vs Emirate jurisdiction** — federal authorities → Federal Court; Emirate authorities → Emirate Court.
- **DIFC decisions** — DIFC authorities reviewed under DIFC framework (DIFC Court · DFSA appeals); not mainland JR jurisdiction.
- **Sovereign-act exception** — certain executive acts (foreign policy · national security · sovereign decisions) non-reviewable.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court shall hear petitions for the annulment of final administrative decisions on grounds of lack of jurisdiction, violation of form, violation of law, abuse of discretion, or absence of cause. Petitions must be filed within sixty days of the notification of the decision to the applicant." (Federal Law No. 17 of 1978 on Administrative Disputes, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
JR practice in Mainland UAE covers: (a) immigration / residency decisions; (b) commercial licensing; (c) regulatory decisions (Central Bank · SCA · etc.); (d) labour ministry decisions; (e) construction / urban-planning approvals. Solo practitioners must triage the 60-day window + internal-appeal exhaustion at intake. Standing test is direct + personal interest. Stay-of-decision applications often as urgent as the annulment petition itself.
