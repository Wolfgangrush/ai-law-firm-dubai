## System
## System: DIFC

## Authority Tier
Court Rules (RDC Part 31)

## Language Variants
English (Primary)

## Currency Warning
DIFC expert evidence framework under RDC Part 31 broadly follows English CPR Part 35. Permission of Court required to call expert evidence (RDC 31.4). Expert's primary duty is to the Court (RDC 31.2) — overrides obligation to instructing party.

## Template Type
Application + Expert Letter of Instruction + Expert Report Template

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) APPLICATION FOR PERMISSION TO ADDUCE EXPERT EVIDENCE**

1. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Application for Permission to Adduce Expert Evidence under RDC 31.4".
3. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Permission to adduce expert evidence on [identified issues].
   - Identification of proposed expert + field of expertise.
   - Time-limit for service of expert report.
   - Costs.
4. **Grounds** — expert evidence reasonably required to resolve proceedings.

**(B) EXPERT LETTER OF INSTRUCTION**

1. Letterhead of instructing solicitor.
2. **Confidentiality recital** — communication subject to legal advice + litigation privilege.
3. **Engagement basis** — instruction to provide expert evidence on specified issues.
4. **Expert's overriding duty to Court** (RDC 31.2) — *"Your overriding duty under RDC Part 31 is to the Court, overriding any obligation to the party instructing you or paying you. Please confirm acceptance in writing."*
5. **Background facts** — concise factual matrix.
6. **Questions for the Expert** — numbered list (3-10 questions).
7. **Materials enclosed** — pleadings · documents · prior reports · industry standards.
8. **Format required** — RDC 31.10-compliant report.
9. **Fees + timeline + closing**.

**(C) EXPERT REPORT (RDC 31.10 compliant)**

1. **Section 1 — Expert qualifications + experience**.
2. **Section 2 — Acknowledgment of overriding duty to Court** (RDC 31.2 + 31.10):
   - Verbatim acknowledgment + signature + date.
3. **Section 3 — Instructions received** + materials reviewed.
4. **Section 4 — Facts assumed** vs facts independently verified.
5. **Section 5 — Investigations conducted** (tests · measurements · site visits).
6. **Section 6 — Opinions** with reasons + supporting literature.
7. **Section 7 — Matters of uncertainty** acknowledged.
8. **Section 8 — Statement of Truth** (RDC 31.10) — verbatim acknowledgment.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Permission required (RDC 31.4)** — engaging expert without permission risks not being permitted to adduce evidence.
- **Single Joint Expert (SJE)** — court may direct under RDC 31.7.
- **Joint Expert Meetings** — court may direct under RDC 31.6; experts confer + produce joint statement narrowing issues; without prejudice.
- **Expert's primary duty to Court** — paramount; failure to acknowledge in report renders evidence inadmissible.
- **Concurrent evidence (hot-tubbing)** — increasingly used at DIFC Court; experts examined together.
- **Cross-examination** — both parties have right to cross-examine.
- **Cost of expert** — borne by instructing party; reallocated in final costs.
- **Updating reports** — supplementary report permitted if material change in circumstances.
- **Reporting on credibility** — expert may NOT opine on credibility of witnesses (RDC 31.11).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"It is the duty of an expert to help the Court on matters within his expertise. This duty overrides any obligation to the person from whom he has received instructions or by whom he is paid." (RDC Rule 31.2, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
DIFC expert evidence framework is closer to English CPR than mainland Khabir system. Solo practitioners must (a) apply for permission to call expert evidence; (b) instruct expert with overriding-duty acknowledgment; (c) ensure report complies with RDC 31.10 format. Hot-tubbing increasingly common at DIFC Court — prepare expert for concurrent format.
