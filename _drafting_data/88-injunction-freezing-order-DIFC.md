## Currency Warning
Freezing Order (Mareva injunction) under RDC Part 25.27. Applicant must show (a) good arguable case on merits; (b) defendant has assets within or outside jurisdiction; (c) real risk of dissipation. Worldwide Mareva available subject to *Cardile*-equivalent DIFC principles. Almost always commenced without notice; full inter partes return date within 7-14 days.

## Template Type
Application + Supporting Witness Statement + Draft Freezing Order

## Structural Skeleton

**Application Notice** (without notice / ex parte)

1. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Application for Freezing Order".
3. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Order restraining respondent from disposing of, dealing with, or diminishing the value of, assets within DIFC [/ Dubai Mainland via DIFC-Dubai Protocol / worldwide] up to value of USD `<amount>`.
   - Ancillary disclosure order — respondent to disclose all assets within 7 days.
   - Liberty for respondent to apply on 48 hours' notice.
   - Liberty for third parties (banks) to apply.
   - Costs reserved.
4. **Undertaking as to damages** offered + fortified if applicant's capacity doubted.
5. **Penal notice** mandatory.

**Supporting Witness Statement**

1. **Deponent** — applicant / authorised representative.
2. **Good arguable case** — detailed pleading of underlying claim + documentary evidence of liability.
3. **Respondent's assets** — bank account references · DIFC company shares · real estate (DIFC + onshore) · listed companies of which respondent is director / shareholder · cryptocurrency holdings.
4. **Real risk of dissipation** — evidence: prior unexplained asset transfers · dishonesty · fraud allegations · evidence of asset relocation · history of evasion.
5. **Worldwide Mareva grounds** (if sought) — DIFC assets insufficient · respondent's evasion · *Cardile*-equivalent principles.
6. **Cross-undertaking as to damages** — applicant's solvency / fortification.
7. **Full and frank disclosure** — paramount; disclose any weakness.
8. **Annexures** — contracts · correspondence · banking documents · DIFC Registrar of Companies searches · onshore RDC searches · evidence of dissipation.
9. **Statement of Truth** (RDC 22).

**Draft Freezing Order** — DIFC Court standard form annexed:

1. Penal notice.
2. Specific assets identified up to USD `<amount>`.
3. Carve-outs:
   - Ordinary living expenses (specified weekly amount).
   - Ordinary business expenses (specified monthly amount).
   - Legal expenses for defending proceedings.
4. Disclosure order (assets list within 7 days).
5. Service mode + timing.
6. Return date for inter partes hearing.
7. Liberty to apply on 48 hours' notice.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Without notice mandatory** in practice — otherwise respondent dissipates.
- **Full and frank disclosure duty** paramount.
- **Penal notice** prominent.
- **Carve-outs** specified at time of grant.
- **Service** within 7 days; respondent has right to apply to discharge.
- **Worldwide Mareva** — DIFC courts cautious; require strong evidence of cross-border dissipation pattern.
- **Cross-undertaking fortification** — common where respondent's business reputation at stake.
- **DIFC-Dubai Protocol** — DIFC freezing orders enforced against onshore Dubai assets without re-litigation; rapid execution.
- **Coordinated injunctions** — for assets in multiple jurisdictions, coordinate with foreign courts.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court may grant an interim injunction restraining a defendant from disposing of, dealing with, or diminishing the value of his assets pending judgment, where the applicant has a good arguable case, the defendant has assets within or outside the jurisdiction, and there is a real risk that the defendant may dissipate those assets so as to render any judgment against him nugatory." (RDC Part 25.27, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The principal asset-preservation tool in DIFC fraud, breach-of-trust, and high-value contract claims. Solo practitioners must run 48-72 hour drill: gather documentary evidence + DIFC Registrar searches + asset trace + draft witness statement + brief counsel. The full-and-frank-disclosure obligation is the principal trap. DIFC-Dubai Protocol enables rapid execution against onshore Dubai assets — significant advantage over pure-onshore precautionary attachment (file 26).
