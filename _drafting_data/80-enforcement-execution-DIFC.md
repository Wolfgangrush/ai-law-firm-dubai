English (Primary)

## Currency Warning
DIFC judgments are enforced (a) within DIFC: directly by DIFC Court enforcement procedures; (b) onshore Dubai: through Dubai Courts Execution Court under the DIFC-Dubai Enforcement Protocol 2014 (as amended); (c) other UAE Emirates: via Federal cooperation; (d) internationally: via reciprocal enforcement treaties + NYC for arbitral awards.

## Template Type
Application / Procedural Document

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) APPLICATION FOR EXECUTION WITHIN DIFC**

1. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties (now Judgment Creditor + Judgment Debtor).
2. **Title** — "Application for Order of Execution".
3. **Identification of judgment** — date · sum awarded · interest · costs.
4. **Sum remaining due** — principal + accrued interest + costs - partial payments.
5. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Order of execution against specified assets.
   - Liberty for Marshal to seize + sell.
   - Costs of execution added to judgment debt.
6. **Annexures** — sealed copy of judgment · interest computation · evidence of assets.

**(B) DIFC-DUBAI ENFORCEMENT PROTOCOL** (for onshore enforcement)

1. **Application to Dubai Court Execution Court** to enforce DIFC judgment.
2. **Translation requirement** — judgment translated to Arabic by court-recognised translator.
3. **Certification** — DIFC Court certifies judgment is final + enforceable.
4. **Execution Court procedure** — Dubai Courts treat DIFC judgment as equivalent to Dubai Court judgment.
5. **Recognition** — no re-litigation of merits; only procedural / public policy challenges available.

**(C) ENFORCEMENT MECHANISMS** (within DIFC + onshore via Protocol)

1. **Order of Execution against goods** — Marshal seizes movable property.
2. **Garnishee** (Third-Party Debt Order) — see file 81.
3. **Charging Order** — attach interests in shares · land · trusts.
4. **Examination of Judgment Debtor** — debtor examined under oath about means.
5. **Sequestration** — for non-monetary orders.
6. **Committal for Contempt** — for breach of injunction / restraint orders.

## Drafting Conventions
- **DIFC currency** — judgments typically in USD or AED; execution in either.
- **DIFC-Dubai Protocol** — streamlined cross-border execution within Dubai; no Article 9 (Federal Decree-Law 42/2022) re-examination.
- **International enforcement** — DIFC judgments enforceable in:
  - Reciprocal-treaty states (GCC · India · France · UK certain conditions).
  - NYC for arbitral awards seated in DIFC.
  - Hague Convention on Choice of Court Agreements (UAE acceded — DIFC judgments may benefit).
- **Asset trace** — pre-execution identification of assets via banking · Land Registry · DIFC company registry.
- **No equivalent to PPSR** — security interests registered separately under DIFC Insolvency Law + onshore Federal Movable Assets Mortgage Law.
- **Limitation on execution** — 15 years for DIFC judgments under DIFC Court Law + Limitation Law.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court has jurisdiction to enforce its judgments and orders by such means as are appropriate, including execution against goods, attachment of debts, charging orders, examination of judgment debtors, and orders for sequestration or committal." (RDC Part 45, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
DIFC execution is procedurally straightforward; the principal commercial value is the DIFC-Dubai Enforcement Protocol enabling DIFC judgments to be enforced against onshore Dubai assets without re-litigation. Solo practitioners advising creditor clients should identify both DIFC and onshore Dubai asset locations pre-judgment; the Protocol opens onshore execution rapidly.
