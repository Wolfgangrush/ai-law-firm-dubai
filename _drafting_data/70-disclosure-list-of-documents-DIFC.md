## Currency Warning
DIFC Disclosure regime under RDC Part 28 broadly follows English CPR Part 31. Standard Disclosure (Rule 28.18) requires documents on which the party relies AND documents that adversely affect the party's own case OR support another party's case. Extended Disclosure available by order.

## Template Type
Procedural Document / Disclosure List

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · Court of First Instance · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "List of Documents".
3. **Part 1 — Documents in the party's possession**:
   - Section A: Documents the party will rely upon at trial.
   - Section B: Documents the party is required to disclose under RDC 28.18 (Standard Disclosure).
4. **Part 2 — Documents previously in possession** (no longer in possession; when disposed of; current location if known).
5. **Part 3 — Documents claimed to be privileged** (see file 71).
6. **Disclosure Statement** — signed by the party (not solicitor) per RDC 28.21:
   - Description of search conducted.
   - Why search proportionate.
   - Certificate that disclosure obligation understood.
7. **Date + Signature**.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Standard Disclosure** (Rule 28.18) — documents party relies upon + documents adversely affecting own case OR supporting another party's case.
- **Reasonable search** — search must be proportionate to issues in dispute.
- **Continuing obligation** — duty continues throughout proceedings.
- **Disclosure Statement** signed by the party personally per RDC 28.21 — not solicitor.
- **Electronic documents** — emails, chat logs, cloud documents, native format preserved.
- **Specific Disclosure** — application under RDC 28.30 where specific documents needed.
- **Privilege carve-out** — see file 71 privilege log.
- **Inspection** — opposing party entitled to inspect listed non-privileged documents per RDC 28.16-17.
- **Sanctions for non-compliance** — adverse inferences, strike-out, costs.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"An order to give standard disclosure requires a party to disclose only (a) the documents on which he relies; and (b) the documents which adversely affect his own case; or adversely affect another party's case; or support another party's case." (RDC Rule 28.18, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
List of Documents is the gateway disclosure step in DIFC litigation. Solo practitioners must maintain meticulous document inventories from intake. The Disclosure Statement signed by the party (not solicitor) is the principal procedural compliance point. Electronic discovery (eDiscovery) increasingly common; pre-action client briefing on document preservation essential.
