## Currency Warning
Without-notice (ex parte) interim injunctions are the principal urgent remedy in DIFC. Strict full-and-frank disclosure obligation; failure leads to discharge regardless of merits.

## Template Type
Application + Supporting Witness Statement + Draft Orders

## Structural Skeleton

**Application Notice** (without notice / ex parte)

1. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Application for Without-Notice Interim Injunction".
3. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Interim injunction restraining respondent from `<specified conduct>` until further order.
   - Liberty to apply on 48 hours' notice.
   - Inter partes return date within 7-14 days.
   - Costs reserved.
4. **Undertaking as to damages** offered.
5. **Penal notice**.

**Supporting Witness Statement**

1. **Deponent** — applicant / authorised representative.
2. **Urgency** — why ex parte rather than inter partes:
   - Imminent threat (timing pressure).
   - Risk of respondent thwarting relief if notice given.
   - Specific evidence of intent / capability.
3. **Serious question to be tried** (RDC Part 25 + *American Cyanamid* / *ABC v O'Neill*-equivalent).
4. **Balance of convenience** — harm to applicant from refusal vs harm to respondent from grant.
5. **Inadequacy of damages**.
6. **Full and frank disclosure** — disclosure of matters that may weigh against grant.
7. **Annexures** — contracts · correspondence · evidence of imminent harm.
8. **Statement of Truth**.

**Draft Orders**

1. Identify conduct restrained with precision.
2. Carve-outs (Mareva carve-outs file 88; restraint-of-trade carve-outs).
3. Penal notice attached.
4. Return date for inter partes hearing.
5. Liberty for respondent to apply on 48 hours' notice.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Without-notice is the exception** — DIFC Court prefers inter partes; ex parte requires demonstrated risk.
- **Full and frank disclosure duty** paramount.
- **Penal notice** mandatory for committal exposure.
- **Carve-outs** essential — ordinary expenses · legal fees · in Mareva context.
- **Inter partes return** — within 7-14 days; applicant must convert to inter partes order or it lapses.
- **Service** within 7 days of order on respondent.
- **Genuine attempt to give notice** — even where without-notice sought, applicant should document attempts.
- **Coordinated with Mainland onshore** — if respondent's assets onshore, coordinate with DIFC-Dubai Protocol execution.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court may grant interim injunctions by interlocutory order where there is a serious question to be tried, the balance of convenience favours the grant, and damages are an inadequate remedy. Where the application is made without notice to the respondent, the applicant must give full and frank disclosure of all matters relevant to the application." (RDC Part 25 + common-law principles, synthesised.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The without-notice injunction is the principal urgent remedy in DIFC where the respondent would otherwise frustrate the relief — typical scenarios are imminent disposal of assets, imminent publication of confidential information, imminent breach of restraint-of-trade clauses. Solo practitioners must move FAST: 48-72 hour drill — gather evidence + draft witness statement + brief counsel + secure duty-judge hearing. The full-and-frank-disclosure obligation is the principal trap — disclose every weakness in the case to the ex parte judge.
