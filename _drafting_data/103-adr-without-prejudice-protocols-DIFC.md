## Currency Warning
DIFC settlement-offer regimes: (a) Without-Prejudice (WP) under common-law principles; (b) Without-Prejudice Save As To Costs (WPSATC / Calderbank-equivalent); (c) **Part 36 Offer** — formal offer under RDC Part 36 with automatic costs consequences on non-acceptance.

## Template Type
Procedural Document / Offer Letter

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) WITHOUT-PREJUDICE LETTER**

1. Letterhead.
2. **Marking** — "WITHOUT PREJUDICE" prominently at top.
3. **Date · Addressee** — opposing solicitor.
4. **Re** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties.
5. **Recital** — *"This letter is written WITHOUT PREJUDICE and is subject to without-prejudice privilege."*
6. **Offer terms** — substantive settlement proposal.
7. **Deadline for acceptance** — reasonable period (7-21 days).
8. **Signature**.

**(B) CALDERBANK OFFER (WPSATC)**

1. **Marking** — "WITHOUT PREJUDICE SAVE AS TO COSTS".
2. **Recital** — *"This letter is written without prejudice save as to costs. It may be used by either party as to costs orders if the substantive proceedings are determined less favourably to the recipient than the terms of this offer."*
3. **Offer terms** — clear, unambiguous.
4. **Deadline** — 14-28 days.
5. **Costs consequences** — reservation of right to seek indemnity costs from offer date.
6. **Signature**.

**(C) RDC PART 36 OFFER**

1. **Form** — RDC Part 36 Offer letter.
2. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties.
3. **Title** — "Part 36 Offer".
4. **Offer terms** — payment · non-monetary obligations · costs proposed.
5. **Relevant period** — minimum 21 days.
6. **Filing** — with Registrar; served on opposing party.
7. **Costs consequences** (RDC Part 36):
   - If claimant offers + obtains judgment ≥ offer → claimant entitled to indemnity costs from offer date + enhanced interest.
   - If defendant offers + claimant fails to obtain judgment > offer → defendant entitled to indemnity costs from offer date.

**(D) DRAFTING DISTINCTIONS**

| Feature | WP | WPSATC (Calderbank) | RDC Part 36 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Privilege scope | Full inadmissibility | Inadmissible on merits; admissible on costs | Inadmissible on merits; automatic costs consequences |
| Costs consequence | None | Discretionary indemnity costs | Mandatory unless court otherwise orders |
| Marking | "WITHOUT PREJUDICE" | "WPSATC" | RDC Part 36 form |
| Filing | Not filed | Not filed | Filed with Registrar |
| Use at trial | Cannot | Tendered for costs only | Tendered for costs (mandatory regime) |

## Drafting Conventions
- **Marking is critical** — failure to mark may forfeit privilege.
- **Genuine offer** — capable of acceptance.
- **Clear terms** — vague offers fail Calderbank consequences.
- **Reasonable deadline** — 7-21 days for WP; 14-28 days for Calderbank; minimum 21 days for RDC Part 36.
- **Costs reservation** — explicit in Calderbank.
- **Lump-sum offers** — typically include all heads of damage + interest + costs; structured offers permissible.
- **Strategic use** — Calderbank + RDC Part 36 commonly used in tandem for maximum costs leverage.
- **Best timing** — early enough to give respondent meaningful opportunity.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A party who has obtained judgment which is at least as advantageous to him as the proposals contained in a Part 36 offer made by that party is entitled to interest on the whole or part of any sum of money (excluding interest) awarded to him, at a rate not exceeding 10% above DIFC base rate for some or all of the period starting with the date on which the relevant period expired." (RDC Part 36, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The three settlement-offer regimes are the principal litigation-leverage tools in DIFC. Solo practitioners should triage early: (a) WP for exploratory negotiations; (b) Calderbank for serious settlement attempts with cost-protection objective; (c) RDC Part 36 for automatic costs-consequence leverage. Combined use of Calderbank + RDC Part 36 common in heavily-contested matters.
