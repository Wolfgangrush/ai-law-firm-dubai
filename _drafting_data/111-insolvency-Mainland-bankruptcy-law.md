## Currency Warning
Mainland UAE Bankruptcy Law modernised by Federal Decree-Law 51/2023 (replacing 9/2016). New procedures: (a) **Preventive Settlement** — court-supervised pre-insolvency restructuring; (b) **Restructuring** — court-supervised reorganisation for distressed but viable businesses; (c) **Bankruptcy** — liquidation + distribution; (d) **Composition with Creditors** — informal arrangement. Available for corporate + individual debtors operating commercial business.

## Template Type
Application + Supporting Documents

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) PREVENTIVE SETTLEMENT APPLICATION** (التسوية الواقية)

1. **Heading** — Court of First Instance · Dubai Courts · case number · In the Matter of `<Company>`.
2. **Title** — "Application for Preventive Settlement under Federal Decree-Law 51/2023".
3. **Applicant** — debtor company itself (Preventive Settlement is debtor-initiated).
4. **Grounds**:
   - Anticipated inability to pay debts but not yet insolvent.
   - Realistic prospect of restructuring.
5. **Restructuring plan** — proposed treatment of creditors.
6. **Moratorium** — court orders stay of creditor actions during process.
7. **Court trustee** appointed to supervise.

**(B) RESTRUCTURING APPLICATION** (إعادة الهيكلة)

1. **Application** by debtor or creditor.
2. **Grounds**:
   - Inability to pay debts.
   - Realistic prospect of survival as going concern.
3. **Restructuring plan** — creditor-class treatment.
4. **Creditor vote** — class-by-class.
5. **Court approval** of plan.

**(C) BANKRUPTCY APPLICATION** (الإفلاس)

1. **Application** by:
   - Debtor itself (self-declared bankruptcy).
   - Creditor (after demonstrated default).
   - Court (in certain circumstances).
   - Public Prosecution (in case of fraudulent activity).
2. **Grounds**:
   - Inability to pay debts.
   - Insolvency (assets less than liabilities).
3. **Court declaration of bankruptcy** + appointment of Trustee in Bankruptcy.
4. **Distribution** — statutory priorities:
   - Secured creditors (up to security value).
   - Preferred creditors (employees · tax · social security).
   - Ordinary unsecured creditors (pro-rata).
   - Subordinated creditors.

**(D) NEW REGIME FEATURES** (Federal Decree-Law 51/2023)

1. **Director's safe-harbour** — directors may avoid personal liability if action taken with reasonable expectation of better outcome.
2. **Creditor class voting** — class-based; 75% threshold within class.
3. **Cross-class cram-down** — class that votes against may be bound if conditions met.
4. **Cross-border insolvency** — UNCITRAL Model Law principles incorporated.
5. **Pre-pack restructuring** — informal pre-court restructuring with subsequent court approval.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Arabic mandatory** — all filings in Arabic.
- **Specialist Insolvency Courts** — Dubai Courts have dedicated Insolvency Department.
- **Coordinated with DIFC** where group structure spans both jurisdictions.
- **Director's personal liability** — Federal Decree-Law 51/2023 introduced safe-harbour; document actions taken with reasonable expectation.
- **Public Prosecution role** — for fraudulent bankruptcy; criminal investigation parallel to civil insolvency.
- **Composition with creditors** — informal alternative for smaller debts.
- **Cross-border recognition** — UNCITRAL Model Law framework.
- **Limitation on petitions** — 30-day notice requirement for creditor petitions; debtor self-declaration permitted any time.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A debtor may apply for a preventive settlement before the onset of insolvency where there is a realistic prospect of restructuring the debts of the debtor and continuing the business. The court may order a moratorium on creditor actions during the preventive settlement process." (Federal Decree-Law No. 51 of 2023, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The new Federal Decree-Law 51/2023 framework substantially modernises Mainland UAE insolvency. Solo practitioners advising distressed corporate clients should consider Preventive Settlement (pre-insolvency) or Restructuring (post-insolvency) over outright Bankruptcy. Director safe-harbour is a significant change; document decisions taken with reasonable expectation of better outcome. Cross-border coordination with DIFC + foreign insolvency proceedings via UNCITRAL Model Law principles.
