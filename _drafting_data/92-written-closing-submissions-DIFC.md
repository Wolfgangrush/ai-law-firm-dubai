## System
## System: DIFC

## Authority Tier
Court Rules (RDC Part 39) + DIFC Court Practice Directions

## Language Variants
English (Primary)

## Currency Warning
Written closing submissions filed AFTER evidence closes, typically within 14-21 days. They are the principal vehicle for the judgment — DIFC judges write reserved judgments largely on basis of written closings.

## Template Type
Submission

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Cover sheet** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties · counsel · date · page count · word count.
2. **Heading** — "Written Closing Submissions of the [Claimant / Defendant]".
3. **Index of contents**.
4. **Executive summary** (½-1 page) — party's case in one tight paragraph + result sought.
5. **Procedural background** (½ page) — pleadings · interlocutory orders · trial dates · witnesses.
6. **Trial issues** — bulleted (3-7 issues).
7. **Burden + standard of proof**:
   - Civil trial: balance of probabilities (DIFC follows English standard).
   - Fraud allegations: higher quality of evidence (*Three Rivers (No 3)* principles).
8. **Witnesses called + credibility**:
   - For each witness:
     - Name + role.
     - Witness Statement paragraphs relied upon.
     - Cross-examination breakdowns (transcript page + line).
     - Credibility submission.
9. **Documentary evidence weighed** — contemporaneous documents preferred over witness recollection.
10. **For each trial issue**:
    - Pleaded case.
    - Evidence adduced.
    - Findings of fact sought.
    - Legal framework — DIFC law · DIFC Common Law · applicable statute.
    - Application of law to findings.
    - Conclusion on issue.
11. **Quantum**:
    - Heads of loss · methodology · interest under DIFC Court Rate · costs.
12. **Costs**:
    - Basis (standard · indemnity).
    - Scale (DIFC Court fee scales).
    - Calderbank offers (if relevant).
13. **Disposition sought**:
    - Judgment for `<sum>` against `<defendant>` with interest.
    - Declarations.
    - Injunctions.
    - Costs.
14. **Form of judgment** annexed — short paragraph form for Court.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Page limits** — typically 80-150 pages for 5-day trial; verify Practice Direction.
- **Pinpoint citations**:
  - Trial Bundle (TB pp 234-237).
  - Witness Statements (WS of [Witness] paras 12-15).
  - Transcript (T 234/15).
  - Authorities (*Three Rivers (No 3)* [2003] 2 AC 1 at [55]).
- **OSCOLA citation** common but DIFC accepts variants.
- **Quote sparingly**.
- **Concede minor issues** — focus on determinative.
- **Address every key opposing-evidence point** — silence signals weakness.
- **Cross-examination breakdowns** — quote operative exchanges.
- **No new evidence in closings**.
- **Reply submissions** — permitted within short window; limited to new matters.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"After the close of evidence, parties shall file written closing submissions in accordance with the directions of the Court. The submissions shall identify the issues, summarise the evidence, set out the legal arguments, and identify the relief sought." (DIFC Court Practice Direction, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The written closing is THE document of the trial — judgments are written largely from it. Solo practitioners should allocate substantial post-trial time (3-7 working days for complex case). Common mistakes: (a) re-presenting Witness Statement narrative without engaging with evidence given; (b) failing to address opposing party's strongest documents; (c) treating quantum as an afterthought.
