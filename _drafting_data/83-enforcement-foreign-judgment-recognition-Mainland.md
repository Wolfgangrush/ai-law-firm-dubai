## Currency Warning
Mainland UAE foreign-judgment recognition under Federal Decree-Law 42/2022 Chapter 5 (replacing Federal Law 11/1992). Foreign judgments enforced subject to reciprocity + public policy + jurisdictional checks. UAE acceded to: NYC (arbitral awards) · Hague Service Convention · Hague Apostille Convention · GCC Reciprocal Convention.

## Template Type
Originating Application

## Structural Skeleton

**Application for Recognition + Enforcement of Foreign Judgment**

1. **Heading** — Court of First Instance · Dubai Courts · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Application for Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Judgment".
3. **Foreign judgment particulars**:
   - Court of origin (full name).
   - Date · case number · operative orders.
   - Finality + non-appealability evidence.
   - Whether judgment has been (or is being) satisfied in country of origin.
4. **Reciprocity basis** — one of:
   - **Bilateral treaty** — UAE has treaties with India · France · Egypt · China · UK · etc.
   - **GCC Reciprocal Convention** — GCC Member State judgments.
   - **Common-law reciprocity** — countries with reciprocal enforcement of UAE judgments.
5. **Jurisdiction check** — foreign court had jurisdiction per UAE rules.
6. **Public policy compliance** — judgment does not violate UAE public policy:
   - Islamic Sharia principles (for family + inheritance matters).
   - UAE Constitutional rights.
   - UAE statutes of mandatory application.
7. **Service evidence** — judgment debtor was duly served in foreign proceedings.
8. **No conflicting UAE judgment** — no prior UAE judgment between same parties on same subject.
9. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Recognition of foreign judgment.
   - Order of execution.
   - Costs.
10. **Annexures**:
    - Foreign judgment (legalised + Arabic translation by court-recognised translator).
    - Apostille / legalisation chain.
    - Confirmation of finality (foreign court certificate).
    - Service evidence in foreign proceedings.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Apostille / Legalisation chain** — for non-Hague Apostille Convention countries:
  - Notarisation in country of origin.
  - Authentication by foreign affairs ministry.
  - Authentication by UAE Embassy abroad.
  - Authentication by UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs.
- **Arabic translation** — by court-recognised legal translator (مترجم قانوني معتمد).
- **Reciprocity assessment** — case-by-case; UAE courts examine practice of foreign court toward UAE judgments.
- **NYC awards** — arbitral awards seated abroad enforced under separate NYC regime; faster than foreign-judgment recognition.
- **GCC judgments** — streamlined under GCC Reciprocal Convention (1996); minimal procedural barriers.
- **DIFC-Dubai Protocol** — DIFC judgments enforced under separate Protocol; faster than foreign-judgment route.
- **Public policy challenges** — common ground for resistance; particularly:
  - Interest-bearing judgments (Sharia objections in some contexts; commercial interest generally permitted).
  - Family matters with non-Sharia compliance.
- **Limitation** — 15 years for foreign-judgment recognition action (Federal Civil Code).
- **No re-litigation of merits** — UAE court does not review substance of foreign judgment.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Foreign judgments shall be recognised and enforced in the State subject to the conditions of reciprocity, that the foreign court had jurisdiction over the matter, that the parties were duly served and represented, that the judgment is final and conclusive, that there is no conflicting judgment of UAE courts, and that the judgment does not violate UAE public policy or morals." (Federal Decree-Law No. 42 of 2022, Article 222, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Foreign-judgment enforcement in Mainland UAE is increasingly streamlined post-2022 reforms. Solo practitioners advising international creditor clients must (a) verify reciprocity basis pre-application; (b) prepare legalisation + apostille chain; (c) obtain court-recognised Arabic translation; (d) anticipate public-policy challenges. DIFC-Dubai Protocol bypasses foreign-judgment recognition for DIFC Court judgments; NYC bypasses it for foreign arbitral awards.
