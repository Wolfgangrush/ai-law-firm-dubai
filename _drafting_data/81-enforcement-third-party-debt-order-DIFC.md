English (Primary)

## Currency Warning
Third Party Debt Order (TPDO) under RDC Part 49 (DIFC equivalent of English Garnishee Order). Two-stage: (1) Interim TPDO (without notice to debtor); (2) Final TPDO (inter partes).

## Template Type
Two-stage Application

## Structural Skeleton

**Stage 1 — Interim Third Party Debt Order (Without Notice)**

*Application Notice + Supporting Witness Statement*

1. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties (Judgment Creditor + Judgment Debtor + Third Party).
2. **Title** — "Application for Interim Third Party Debt Order".
3. **Reliefs sought**:
   - All debts due or accruing due from the third party `<name + address>` to the judgment debtor attached up to value of USD `<amount>` + interest + costs.
   - Third party to attend hearing on `<date>` to show cause why order should not be final.
   - In meantime, third party restrained from paying the debtor.
4. **Supporting Witness Statement**:
   - Judgment + balance outstanding.
   - Existence of debt — evidence third party owes debtor (bank statement · invoice · employment contract · lease).
   - Sum or estimated sum held by third party.
   - Service intentions.
5. **Annexures** — judgment · cost certificate · evidence of debt.
6. **Statement of Truth**.

**Stage 2 — Final Third Party Debt Order (Inter Partes)**

- Hearing notice served on third party + debtor.
- Third party may file evidence disputing liability.
- Debtor may file evidence objecting (third-party claims · exempt funds · payment already made).
- Court makes order final if debt established; third party directed to pay creditor.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Bank account targeting** — pre-identification of bank accounts essential.
- **DIFC + onshore banks** — TPDO against DIFC-located bank branches direct; onshore banks via DIFC-Dubai Protocol.
- **Joint accounts** — only debtor's beneficial interest attachable; complications.
- **Salary attachments** — subject to UAE Labour Law minimum wage protections.
- **Trust account exemption** — solicitor's trust account not attachable for client's unrelated debts.
- **Service** — Interim TPDO served on third party + debtor; minimum 7 clear days before return date.
- **Cost of TPDO process** added to judgment debt.
- **Foreign-currency conversion** — DIFC default at AED/USD spot rate on order date.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Where a person has obtained a judgment for the payment of a sum of money, the Court may make an order requiring a third party who is indebted to the judgment debtor to pay to the judgment creditor the amount of any debt due or accruing due from the third party to the judgment debtor up to the amount of the judgment debt." (RDC Part 49, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
TPDO on bank accounts is the most efficient post-judgment recovery tool in DIFC — typically catches funds within days. Solo practitioners should obtain banking discovery during the underlying proceedings to maximise the chance of hitting an account with funds. Combine with charging order + EJD for full coverage.
