## System
## System: SHARED (DIFC + Mainland + International)

## Authority Tier
International: Singapore Convention on Mediation (in force globally August 2020; UAE in force March 2021) + DIFC RDC Part 27 + Federal Law 17/2016

## Language Variants
English (DIFC + international) · Arabic (Mainland enforcement)

## Currency Warning
UAE acceded to Singapore Convention August 2019; in force in UAE 12 March 2021. Convention enables enforcement of international mediated settlement agreements in Contracting States without re-litigation. Significant for cross-border commercial settlements seated in Dubai (whether DIFC or Mainland).

## Template Type
Contract / Settlement Deed

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Parties**:
   - Full identification — natural persons (full names · passport · address) or corporate (full name · UAE commercial license / DIFC company no / foreign registration · registered address).
   - Authorised representatives + capacity (board resolution · power of attorney annexed).
2. **Recitals**:
   - Existence of underlying dispute (without admission of liability).
   - Conduct of mediation — date · Mediator · institutional rules (DIFC-LCIA · DIAC · Dubai Mediation Centre · ad hoc).
   - **"International commercial" character** (for Singapore Convention applicability — parties' places of business in different States OR substantial part of obligations to be performed in different State).
3. **Definitions** — "Effective Date" · "Underlying Claims" · "Released Parties" · "Confidential Information" · "Mediator" · "Mediation Agreement" (file 101).
4. **Settlement Terms**:
   - Payment — amount · currency (AED / USD common) · payment schedule · interest on late payment.
   - Non-monetary obligations — performance · transfer of assets · IP licensing · undertakings.
   - Conditions precedent — regulatory approvals · board approvals.
5. **Mutual Releases**:
   - Full and final settlement of all known + unknown claims arising from subject-matter.
   - Carve-outs (criminal liability · regulatory penalties · fraud · enforcement of agreement).
6. **Discontinuance of Proceedings**:
   - Joint application to court of seizing (DIFC Court / Dubai Courts / DIFC-LCIA / DIAC) to discontinue.
   - Consent order (DIFC) or settlement registration (Dubai Courts) — preserves Court's jurisdiction to enforce.
7. **Confidentiality**:
   - Existence + terms.
   - Limited disclosure carve-outs (auditors · regulators · court enforcement).
8. **No Admission of Liability**.
9. **Singapore Convention Compliance** (for international enforceability):
   - Express statement: *"This agreement results from mediation and is intended to be relied upon under the Singapore Convention on Mediation."*
   - Mediator's signature OR separate signed statement confirming mediation was conducted (Convention Art 4(1)(b)).
   - Written form (Convention Art 1(1)).
   - International character (Convention Art 1(1)).
   - Excluded subject-matter check (Convention Art 1(2) excludes consumer · family · inheritance).
10. **Enforceability Mechanisms**:
    - **DIFC Court consent order** (RDC equivalent of English Tomlin order) for DIFC-court-on-foot mediations.
    - **Dubai Courts notarisation + registration** for Mainland enforceability.
    - **DIAC settlement registration**.
    - **Singapore Convention enforcement** in foreign Contracting States.
11. **Anti-Circumvention** — parties cannot file released claims in another jurisdiction.
12. **Governing Law + Forum** — typically DIFC law or applicable substantive law · forum for disputes arising from agreement.
13. **Boilerplate** — entire agreement · variation in writing · severance · counterparts · electronic execution.
14. **Execution** — formal execution:
    - **DIFC**: signature + witness; deed under seal where required.
    - **Mainland**: notarisation by Notary Public; signatures of authorised representatives with notarised POAs.
    - **International**: per Singapore Convention Art 1(1) "written form" requirement (electronic acceptable).

## Drafting Conventions
- **International character** — required for Singapore Convention; document in recitals.
- **Excluded matters** — consumer · family · inheritance EXCLUDED from Singapore Convention.
- **Mediator's certification** — Convention Art 4(1)(b) — Mediator co-signs OR provides separate signed statement.
- **DIFC consent order** + Singapore Convention compliance — redundant enforceability paths.
- **Mainland notarisation** — required for execution against UAE assets; notary public registers agreement.
- **Tax structuring** — UAE corporate tax + VAT considerations; cross-border withholding.
- **Currency + payment mechanism** — specify currency · bank account · escrow if applicable.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"This Convention applies to an agreement resulting from mediation and concluded in writing by parties to resolve a commercial dispute (international settlement agreement) which, at the time of its conclusion, is international." (Singapore Convention on Mediation 2019, Art 1(1).)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Dubai is positioning as a global hub for mediated cross-border commercial settlements; UAE accession to Singapore Convention is a significant advantage. Solo practitioners advising on cross-border settlements should default to Singapore Convention-compliant structure unless parties' places of business are wholly within a single State. Pair Convention-compliance with DIFC consent order OR Dubai Courts notarisation for redundancy.
