## System: DIFC

Court Rules (RDC Part 50)

English (Primary)

## Currency Warning
Charging Order under RDC Part 50 attaches the judgment debtor's beneficial interest in: (a) shares of DIFC-registered companies; (b) DIFC-registered real estate; (c) interests in DIFC-based trusts. Two-stage: Interim Charging Order (without notice) + Final Charging Order (inter partes).

## Template Type
Two-stage Application

## Structural Skeleton

**Stage 1 — Interim Charging Order (Without Notice)**

1. **Application Notice** + supporting witness statement.
2. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties.
3. **Title** — "Application for Interim Charging Order".
4. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Interim charging order over [shares / real estate / specified asset] of judgment debtor up to USD `<amount>` + interest + costs.
   - Service of order on judgment debtor + interested third parties.
   - Return date for inter partes hearing.
   - Costs.
5. **Witness Statement**:
   - Judgment + outstanding balance.
   - Asset identified — DIFC company shares (with registration details) · land title reference · trust interest.
   - Beneficial ownership of debtor.
   - Service intentions.

**Stage 2 — Final Charging Order (Inter Partes)**

- Service on debtor + interested third parties (co-owners · mortgagees · trustees).
- Hearing to consider objections.
- If final, charge registered:
  - **DIFC company shares** — notice to company + DIFC Registrar of Companies.
  - **Real estate** — caveat / charge registered with DLD (via DIFC-Dubai Protocol if onshore).
- Subsequent sale order under RDC Part 50 for realisation.

## Drafting Conventions
- **DIFC company shares** — registered with DIFC Registrar of Companies (Ras).
- **Real estate** — DIFC-registered freehold + leasehold; onshore via DLD.
- **No charging order on regulatory-licensed entities' assets** without DFSA notification (for DFSA-regulated entities).
- **Family-home considerations** — limited DIFC residential property; protections per DIFC Residential Property Law.
- **Statutory exemptions** — limited; debtor's residential property has some protection if family-occupied.
- **Subsequent sale** — separate order for realisation; equity of redemption preserved until sale.
- **Currency conversion** — foreign-currency judgments registered at exchange rate on registration date.
- **Trusts** — DIFC trust beneficiary interests chargeable subject to trust deed restrictions.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court may, for the purpose of enforcing a judgment or order requiring a person to pay money, make an order imposing on any specified property of the judgment debtor a charge for securing the payment of the judgment debt." (RDC Part 50, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Charging orders are the principal remedy against debtors whose principal asset is DIFC company shares (private companies · regulated entities) or DIFC real estate. Solo practitioners should identify chargeable assets during the underlying proceedings via DIFC Registrar of Companies + DLD searches. Cross-border charging orders via DIFC-Dubai Protocol for onshore property.
