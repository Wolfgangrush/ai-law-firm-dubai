## Currency Warning
Chronology, cast list, and reading list filed alongside opening submissions and Trial Bundle. Orient the bench before evidence opens. Mandatory in complex cases.

## Template Type
Procedural Documents (suite of three)

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) CHRONOLOGY OF EVENTS**

1. Cover sheet — DIFC Courts · case number · parties · counsel · date.
2. Title — "Chronology of Events".
3. Three-column table:

| Date | Event | Reference (TB / Witness Statement) |
|---|---|---|
| YYYY-MM-DD | Claimant incorporated | TB Tab 1, p 3 |
| YYYY-MM-DD | Contract signed | TB Tab 5, p 23 |
| YYYY-MM-DD | First instalment paid | TB Tab 12, p 87 |
| YYYY-MM-DD | Defendant alleged breach | TB Tab 18, p 134; WS of [Witness] paras 12-15 |

4. Date discipline — ISO format · disputed dates flagged · key events bolded.
5. Scope — material events only.

**(B) CAST LIST**

1. Cover sheet.
2. Title — "Cast List of Persons Referred to in the Pleadings and Trial".
3. Table:

| Name | Role | Capacity at material time | Witness for | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mr X | Director of Claimant | Authorised signatory | Claimant | WS dated YYYY-MM-DD |
| Ms Y | Sole shareholder of Defendant | Beneficial owner | Defendant | Not called as witness |
| Mr Z | Auditor | External auditor | Claimant | Expert report dated YYYY-MM-DD |

4. Grouped by side — Claimant's persons · Defendant's persons · neutral.
5. Indicate relationships.

**(C) READING LIST**

1. Cover sheet.
2. Title — "Pre-Trial Reading List for the Court".
3. Estimated reading time — total hours.
4. Recommended pre-trial reading:

| Document | TB Reference | Reading time (min) |
|---|---|---|
| Particulars of Claim | TB Tab 2, p 16-45 | 15 |
| Defence | TB Tab 3, p 46-78 | 15 |
| WS of Claimant's main witness | TB Tab B1, p 200-280 | 45 |
| WS of Defendant's main witness | TB Tab B2, p 281-360 | 45 |
| Contract | TB Tab 5, p 23-40 | 10 |
| Expert report | TB Tab D1, p 600-650 | 30 |
| Skeletal opening submissions | Separately filed | 30 |

5. Total estimated reading time — typically 4-8 hours.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Single document or three separate** — DIFC practice varies; verify with associate.
- **Joint preparation** — parties often jointly prepare; failing agreement, each party files own.
- **Without prejudice to disputes** — chronology marks disputed dates clearly.
- **Tab + page references** mandatory.
- **No advocacy** — orientation documents; arguments in submissions.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Parties shall, prior to trial, file a chronology of events, a cast list of persons, and a recommended reading list for the Court. These documents shall be concise, accurate, and referenced to the Trial Bundle." (DIFC Court Practice Direction, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The chronology and cast list reduce trial-bench confusion; the reading list manages the judge's pre-trial preparation. Solo practitioners often skimp on these — and pay the price in oral-hearing time spent orienting the bench. Investment of 4-6 hours assembling pays back in trial efficiency.
