## System
## System: SHARED (DIFC + Mainland)

## Authority Tier
DIFC: RDC Part 27 + DIFC Mediation Rules · Mainland: Federal Law 17/2016 · International: DIAC Mediation Rules · Singapore Convention

## Language Variants
English (DIFC + DIAC) · Arabic (Mainland Mediation Centre)

## Currency Warning
Mediation agreement governs the procedure + confidentiality. Distinct from mediated settlement (file 102). Must be executed before mediation commences.

## Template Type
Contract Template

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Parties** — all parties + legal representatives + Mediator + accreditation.
2. **Recitals** — dispute identified · parties' desire to mediate · Mediator's appointment.
3. **Mediator's Role + Process**:
   - Neutral facilitator.
   - Facilitative model (default) OR evaluative model (specified).
   - Joint sessions + private caucuses.
   - No determination of merits.
4. **Mediator's Duty + Conflict of Interest**:
   - Independence + impartiality.
   - Disclosure of prior relationships.
   - Withdrawal if conflict arises.
5. **Confidentiality**:
   - All communications confidential.
   - DIFC: common-law without-prejudice + RDC 27 confidentiality.
   - Mainland: Federal Law 17/2016 Art 14.
   - DIAC: DIAC Mediation Rules Art 16.
   - Mediator cannot be compelled to give evidence in subsequent proceedings.
6. **Privilege** — communications inadmissible in subsequent court/arbitration.
7. **Without Prejudice Principle**.
8. **Authority to Settle**:
   - Each party warrants natural-person attending has authority.
   - Corporate parties — board resolution or executive authority.
9. **Mediator's Fees + Cost-sharing**:
   - Fee rate.
   - Allocation — typically 50/50.
   - Payment timing.
10. **Procedure**:
    - Date · venue · format (in-person · video · hybrid).
    - Position papers exchange.
    - Joint session sequence.
    - Caucus availability.
    - Termination on notice.
11. **Outcomes**:
    - Settlement → executed Mediated Settlement Agreement (file 102) + Singapore Convention compliance where international.
    - No settlement → confidentiality continues; mediation does not preclude court resort.
12. **Mediator's Notes** — destroyed at end or retained confidentially.
13. **Liability** — Mediator's limited liability.
14. **Termination** — at any party's election or Mediator's election.
15. **Governing Law + Forum**.
16. **Signatures** — all parties + Mediator + representatives.

## Drafting Conventions
- **DIFC RDC Part 27** + **DIAC Mediation Rules** + **Federal Law 17/2016** — distinct regimes; choose applicable rules.
- **Authority to settle** critical; documented + warranted.
- **Confidentiality scope** clearly drawn.
- **Costs allocation** typically 50/50 initially.
- **Singapore Convention** — flag in agreement if parties want Convention enforceability for resulting settlement.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Mediation is a process in which a neutral third party assists the parties to reach a voluntary settlement of their dispute. The Mediator does not impose a decision. All communications in the mediation are confidential and without prejudice." (DIFC RDC Part 27 + Federal Law 17/2016 + DIAC Mediation Rules, synthesised.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The mediation agreement is the procedural foundation. Solo practitioners often skip or use boilerplate — both risky. Authority-to-settle warranties are the most-litigated post-mediation issue. DIFC + DIAC + Dubai Court Mediation Centre have distinct procedural rules; verify applicable rules in agreement.
