## Last Verified

## System
## System: SHARED (DIFC + Mainland)

## Authority Tier
DIFC: Common-law advisory practice · Mainland: Federal Law 23/1991 + UAE Civil Code

## Language Variants
DIFC: English · Mainland: Arabic (Mandatory for court submissions); English Memos permitted for client advisory

## Currency Warning
Advice on merits is the documented pre-litigation assessment of (a) prospects; (b) quantum; (c) cost-benefit; (d) strategic recommendations. DIFC follows English advisory framework; Mainland advisory more conservative given inquisitorial system + court-appointed expert dominance.

## Template Type
Opinion / Memorandum

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Cover sheet** — counsel/advocate chambers letterhead.
2. **Heading** — "Advice on Merits".
3. **Re** — client · matter · forum (DIFC Court / Dubai Courts / DIFC + Mainland dual-track).
4. **Date · Reference**.
5. **Confidentiality + Privilege recital**:
   - **DIFC**: legal advice + litigation privilege under DIFC common-law principles.
   - **Mainland**: client-advocate confidentiality under Federal Law 23/1991 Art 42 — narrower than DIFC; limited to communications for legal advice.
6. **Instructions received**.
7. **Materials reviewed**.
8. **Background facts**.
9. **Cause(s) of action / defence available**:
   - Each cause / defence identified per applicable law:
     - **DIFC**: DIFC Contract Law 2004 · DIFC Common Law · DIFC Employment Law · etc.
     - **Mainland**: UAE Civil Code (Federal Law 5/1985 + amendments) · Federal Decree-Law 50/2022 (Commercial Transactions) · sector-specific.
   - Application of elements to facts.
   - Prospects of proof — strong / moderate / weak (with reasons).
10. **Anticipated defences / counter-arguments**.
11. **Procedural considerations**:
    - **Forum** — DIFC Court · Dubai Courts · Federal Courts · arbitration (DIAC · LCIA · ICC seated DIFC).
    - **Jurisdiction** — DIFC opt-in jurisdiction OR onshore jurisdiction.
    - **Limitation** — DIFC: 6 years (contract) / 3 years (tort); Mainland: 15 years (general) / 10 years (commercial contracts) / 1 year (tort).
    - **Translation + apostille requirements**.
12. **Quantum analysis**:
    - Heads of loss.
    - Quantification methodology.
    - Likely recoverable range.
    - Defendant's ability to pay.
13. **Strategic options**:
    - DIFC Court vs Dubai Courts forum choice.
    - Mediation through DIAC / DIFC-LCIA.
    - SIAC / ICC arbitration.
    - DIFC-Dubai Enforcement Protocol implications.
14. **Costs estimate**:
    - Pre-action.
    - Issue + pleadings stage.
    - Disclosure stage.
    - Trial.
    - Appeal (DIFC Court of Appeal / Dubai Court of Appeal / Cassation).
    - Recovery prospect.
15. **Recommendations**.
16. **Caveats**.
17. **Signature** — DIFC counsel / Mainland advocate.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Forum selection critical** — DIFC opt-in jurisdiction available for international commercial matters; Dubai Courts for onshore.
- **Limitation comparison** — DIFC short (English-style) vs Mainland longer (civil-law style); choose forum where favourable.
- **Honest assessment** — duty to give plain advice, not advocate.
- **Quantification ranges** preferred to point estimates.
- **Probability framing** — *"good prospects of success" (60-75%)* / *"reasonable prospects" (40-60%)* / *"defensive position only" (<40%)*.
- **Cost-benefit** — DIFC costs typically higher than Mainland; party-party recovery rules differ.
- **Cross-border execution** considerations — DIFC-Dubai Protocol + foreign reciprocity.
- **No guarantees** — both regimes prohibit guarantee of outcome.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"An advocate must give the client an objective assessment of the prospects of the matter, the likely costs, and the strategic options available. The advice shall be honest and competent." (Federal Law 23/1991 + DIFC Code of Conduct, synthesised.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Dual-track Dubai advisory requires sophisticated forum-selection analysis. Solo practitioners advising clients with cross-border exposure should produce parallel DIFC + Mainland assessments + forum-selection recommendation. DIFC opt-in jurisdiction (where contract permits) often preferred for international commercial matters due to common-law procedure + English-language + DIFC-Dubai enforcement.
