## Currency Warning
Set-aside of DIFC default judgment under RDC 13.5. Two distinct grounds: (a) "as of right" — service was defective; (b) "discretionary" — defendant has real prospect of success + reasonable explanation + prompt application.

## Template Type
Application + Supporting Witness Statement

## Structural Skeleton

**Application Notice (RDC Part 23)**

1. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Application to Set Aside Default Judgment".
3. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Default judgment dated `<date>` set aside.
   - Defendant granted leave to file Defence within prescribed period.
   - Stay of execution pending application.
   - Costs.
4. **Grounds**:
   - Defective service (as of right) — OR
   - Real prospect of successfully defending + reasonable explanation for default + prompt application.

**Supporting Witness Statement**

1. **Identification** — defendant or authorised representative.
2. **Explanation for default**:
   - Defective service (didn't receive papers).
   - Wrong address.
   - Communication failure (illness · travel · solicitor error).
   - Administrative oversight.
3. **Real prospect of successfully defending** — substantive grounds:
   - Cause of action contested.
   - Affirmative defences (limitation · accord and satisfaction · setoff).
   - Counterclaim (with quantum).
4. **Prompt application** — explanation of delay between learning of judgment + filing application.
5. **No irremediable prejudice** to claimant — what can be cured by costs.
6. **Conditional relief proposed** — payment into court · security · partial admission.
7. **Annexures**:
   - Proposed Defence (annexed in full).
   - Evidence supporting defence (contemporaneous documents).
   - Affidavit of service (if disputed).
8. **Statement of Truth** (RDC Part 22).

## Drafting Conventions
- **As-of-right set-aside** — defective service triggers automatic set-aside, regardless of merits.
- **Discretionary set-aside** — three-limb test: real prospect of defending + reasonable explanation + prompt application.
- **Proposed Defence substantive** — bare denial + boilerplate insufficient; specific factual contentions required.
- **Promptness** — moves within days/weeks of learning of judgment; multi-month delays scrutinised.
- **Conditional set-aside** — court may impose conditions (security · payment in) where default was without excuse but defence is meritorious.
- **Costs of set-aside** — typically defendant pays claimant's costs of obtaining judgment + costs of set-aside application, on standard basis (indemnity if egregious default).
- **Stay of execution** — file simultaneously to prevent enforcement during hearing.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court must set aside a default judgment if the judgment was wrongly entered because (a) the relevant rules for service were not complied with; or (b) the defendant filed an Acknowledgment of Service or Defence within the period required... The Court may set aside or vary a default judgment if the defendant has a real prospect of successfully defending the claim or it appears to the Court that there is some other good reason." (RDC Rule 13.5, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Set-aside of DIFC default judgment is a high-frequency task for defence-side practice. Common triggers: defendant didn't receive papers (incorrect address) · AoS filed late by 1-2 days · administrative oversight. The "real prospect of success" pleading is the lever — without it, even good reasons for default fail. Solo practitioners should triage default-judgment-served clients within 48 hours to maximise the prompt-application factor.
