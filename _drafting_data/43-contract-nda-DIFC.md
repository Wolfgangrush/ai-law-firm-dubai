## Structural Skeleton
1. Definition of Confidential Information
2. Permitted Purpose
3. Obligations of the Receiving Party
4. Exclusions (Public domain, etc.)
5. Term and Survival
6. Remedies (Injunctions)
7. Governing Law (DIFC)

## Drafting Conventions
- Define "Confidential Information" broadly but reasonably.
- Include "Return or Destroy" provisions.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Receiving Party shall keep the Confidential Information strictly confidential and shall not, without the Disclosing Party's prior written consent, disclose it to any third party."

## Practical Use
Protecting trade secrets during negotiations in the DIFC.
