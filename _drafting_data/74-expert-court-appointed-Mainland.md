## System
## System: Mainland

## Authority Tier
Primary Statute (Federal Decree-Law No. 42 of 2022, Arts 69-92 on Expert Evidence) + Ministry of Justice Expert Roster

## Language Variants
Arabic (Mandatory)

## Currency Warning
Mainland UAE expert-evidence regime is COURT-APPOINTED (al-Khabir al-Qaḍāʾī) — party-appointed experts NOT routinely received. Party may petition court to appoint expert; court selects from Ministry of Justice Expert Roster (Khibrāʾ Mu'tamadūn). Expert report (al-Taqrīr al-Khabīr) is the principal substantive evidence in most commercial disputes.

## Template Type
Procedural Application / Petition

## Structural Skeleton

**Petition for Appointment of Expert** (طلب ندب خبير)

1. **Heading** — Court of First Instance · Dubai Courts · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Petition for Appointment of Court Expert".
3. **Identification of dispute** — pleadings · cause of action · key disputed facts.
4. **Identification of technical question** requiring expert determination:
   - Engineering · construction · IT · accounting · medical · valuation · industry-specific.
5. **Specific questions for the Expert** — numbered list (3-10 questions):
   - Each question precise · self-contained · within proposed expert's specialism.
6. **Proposed Expert** — name from MoJ Expert Roster + specialism.
7. **Expert's fees** — typically borne by applicant initially (court allocates in final judgment).
8. **Materials available to Expert** — pleadings · documents · sites for inspection.
9. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Appointment of named Expert (or court-selected Expert).
   - Time-limit for Expert report.
   - Cost-allocation.

**Expert's Examination Procedure** (post-appointment)

1. **Expert's mandate** documented in court order.
2. **Both parties invited** to attend examination + provide submissions.
3. **Site visits + inspections** — Expert may conduct.
4. **Expert's draft report** circulated to parties for comments.
5. **Final Expert Report** filed with court.

**Expert Report Format** (al-Taqrīr al-Khabīr)

1. Cover page (court · case · expert · date).
2. Mandate (questions posed).
3. Materials reviewed.
4. Examinations conducted.
5. Findings + reasons (technical analysis).
6. Answers to court's questions.
7. Signature + seal of Expert.

## Drafting Conventions
- **MoJ Expert Roster** — Ministry of Justice maintains expert roster; court selects from list unless parties agree.
- **Specialism match** — request expert whose specialism matches technical question; mismatched experts disregarded.
- **Expert is the Court's expert** — not the parties' advocate.
- **Expert fees** — first paid by applicant; reallocated by court at judgment.
- **Both parties heard** — Expert must invite both parties to make submissions.
- **Site inspection** — Expert visits relevant sites; parties may attend.
- **Expert's report decisive** in most cases — courts accept findings unless palpably wrong.
- **Challenge to Expert** — party may apply to challenge Expert on grounds of: (a) lack of impartiality; (b) lack of qualification; (c) procedural irregularity in examination.
- **Second Expert** — court may appoint second Expert in case of significant dispute over first report.
- **Translation** — foreign-language documents translated by court-recognised translator for Expert's use.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The court may, on its own motion or on the application of a party, appoint an expert to provide an opinion on technical matters relevant to the dispute. The expert shall be selected from the roster maintained by the Ministry of Justice unless the parties agree otherwise." (Federal Decree-Law No. 42 of 2022, Article 69, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The court-appointed Expert is the principal substantive evidence in mainland UAE commercial litigation — construction · banking · real estate · medical malpractice · IT disputes. Solo practitioners must (a) draft technical questions precisely; (b) propose qualified experts from MoJ Roster; (c) attend examinations + submit position papers; (d) respond promptly to draft report. The court typically accepts Expert findings; challenges succeed only on procedural or qualification grounds.
