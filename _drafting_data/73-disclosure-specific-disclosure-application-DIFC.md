## Currency Warning
Specific Disclosure under RDC 28.30 available where Standard Disclosure inadequate to deal with specific issue. Application by Notice of Application + supporting witness statement.

## Template Type
Court Application + Supporting Witness Statement

## Structural Skeleton

**Application Notice (RDC Part 23 form)**

1. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Application for Specific Disclosure".
3. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Order under RDC 28.30 that opposing party give specific disclosure of documents identified in the schedule annexed.
   - Costs of the application.
4. **Grounds**:
   - Specified documents are or are likely to be in opposing party's control.
   - Specified documents are relevant to issues in pleadings.
   - Specific Disclosure necessary to deal fairly with issues.

**Supporting Witness Statement (RDC Part 32)**

1. **Identification of applicant + role**.
2. **Schedule of documents sought** — categories OR document-by-document:
   - Document type, date range, subject-matter.
   - Anchored to specific issues in pleadings.
3. **Belief documents exist + reasoning** — basis for belief (correspondence references · ordinary business documents).
4. **Why Standard Disclosure inadequate** — opposing party gave inadequate disclosure / did not include responsive documents.
5. **Necessity test** — documents necessary for fair trial of issues.
6. **Proportionality** — search proposed proportionate to issues.

**Schedule of Categories** — annexure with precise categories.

**Draft Order** annexure.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Categories permissible** — but tightly drawn; broad "all communications relating to X" routinely refused.
- **Necessity threshold** — must show Standard Disclosure inadequate.
- **Proportionality** — RDC 28.18 reasonable-search test applies.
- **Cost of search** — applicant may be ordered to bear costs of opposing party's search beyond a baseline.
- **In camera review** — court can inspect contested documents.
- **Privilege carve-out** — privileged documents listed but not produced; see file 71.
- **Electronic search parameters** — keyword + date-range + custodian specifications.
- **Continuing obligation** — applies to documents subsequently coming into possession.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court may make an order for specific disclosure or specific inspection. An order for specific disclosure is an order that a party must do one or more of the following things: (1) disclose documents specified or described in the order; (2) carry out a search to the extent specified in the order; (3) disclose any documents located as a result of that search." (RDC Rule 28.30, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Specific Disclosure is the principal tool against parties giving thin Standard Disclosure. Solo practitioners must identify documents with precision; vague applications refused. Categories should be anchored to specific pleaded issues. Often used where defendant gives bare Standard Disclosure list omitting documents the applicant knows exist from prior correspondence or industry-standard records.
