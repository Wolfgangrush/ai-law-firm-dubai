English (Primary)

## Currency Warning
Trial Bundle preparation mandatory at DIFC Court trials. Applicant prepares; respondent reviews + agrees content. Electronic filing standards apply (searchable PDF · OCR · bookmarks at tabs).

## Template Type
Procedural Document / Bundle Index

## Structural Skeleton

**TRIAL BUNDLE (TB)**

1. **Cover sheet**:
   - DIFC Courts designation.
   - Case number · parties.
   - Volume number (if multi-volume).
   - Counsel · solicitor · date.

2. **Index of contents** — table:
   | Tab | Document | Date | Source | TB Pagination |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | 1 | Claim Form | YYYY-MM-DD | Court file | 1-15 |
   | 2 | Particulars of Claim | YYYY-MM-DD | Court file | 16-45 |
   | 3 | Defence | YYYY-MM-DD | Court file | 46-78 |
   | ... | | | | |

3. **Organisation**:
   - **Section A** — Pleadings (Claim Form · Particulars · Defence · Reply · Counterclaim · amendments).
   - **Section B** — Witness Statements + AEICs.
   - **Section C** — Documents relied upon at trial (chronological within topic).
   - **Section D** — Expert reports (file 75-76).
   - **Section E** — Joint Statements of Experts.
   - **Section F** — Photographs · plans · technical drawings · transcripts.

4. **Pagination** — continuous through TB.

5. **Tab dividers** — electronic bookmarks + physical (if hard copy required).

6. **Joint Trial Bundle** preferred — DIFC Court strongly encourages parties to agree.

7. **Disputed-document protocol** — admissibility disputes addressed in opening submissions or by separate hearing.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Joint TB preferred** — DIFC Court encourages parties to agree single bundle.
- **OSCOLA / DIFC citation** for any case-law indexed.
- **Chronological vs topical** — for documents, chronological in each topic typically clearest.
- **Witness Statement cross-references** — each paragraph that references a document should cite TB pagination.
- **Skeleton cross-references** — written submissions cite TB pagination.
- **Electronic filing standards** — searchable PDF; OCR text layer; bookmarks at tab dividers.
- **Voluminous TBs** — multi-volume; pagination continuous; clear volume markers.
- **Native-format attachments** — audio · video · large spreadsheets as separate exhibits.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Parties shall prepare a Trial Bundle containing all documents to be relied upon at trial. The Trial Bundle shall be paginated, indexed, and filed within the period prescribed by the Court. Parties shall use reasonable efforts to agree the content of the Trial Bundle." (RDC Part 39 + Practice Direction, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The Trial Bundle is the principal physical / electronic artifact of the trial. Solo practitioners must invest significant time in assembly. Joint bundles are the norm in commercial cases. Pagination + indexing are tedious but critical.
