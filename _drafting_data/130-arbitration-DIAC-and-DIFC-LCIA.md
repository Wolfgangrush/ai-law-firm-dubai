## Currency Warning
Dubai arbitration landscape reshaped September 2021 by Decree No. 34 of 2021 merging DIFC-LCIA + EMAC into Dubai International Arbitration Centre (DIAC). DIAC Rules 2022 govern new arbitrations. Pre-merger DIFC-LCIA arbitrations continue under prior rules. **Seat selection critical** — Mainland Dubai seat under Federal Arbitration Law 6/2018; DIFC seat under DIFC Arbitration Law 1/2008.

## Template Type
Arbitration Notice + Procedural Documents

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) ARBITRATION CLAUSE** (in commercial contracts)

Suggested clause (DIAC):
```
"Any dispute, controversy or claim arising out of or relating to this contract,
including the validity, invalidity, breach or termination thereof, shall be
finally settled by arbitration administered by the Dubai International
Arbitration Centre (DIAC) in accordance with its Rules (as in force at the
time of commencement). The seat of the arbitration shall be [DIFC / Dubai
Mainland]. The language of the arbitration shall be [English / Arabic].
The arbitration tribunal shall consist of [one / three] arbitrator(s).
The governing law shall be [DIFC law / UAE law / English law]."
```

**(B) REQUEST FOR ARBITRATION** (DIAC)

1. **Heading** — Dubai International Arbitration Centre.
2. **Title** — "Request for Arbitration".
3. **Claimant** — name · address · representative.
4. **Respondent** — name · address.
5. **Arbitration clause** — verbatim recital + identification of source contract.
6. **Nature of the dispute** — concise summary (1-2 pages).
7. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Declaratory relief.
   - Monetary award (principal · interest · costs).
   - Injunctive relief.
8. **Seat + language + tribunal composition** — per arbitration clause.
9. **Proposed arbitrator** (if sole) or appointment process.
10. **Filing fee** — DIAC scale.
11. **Annexures** — contract · prior correspondence.

**(C) STATEMENT OF CLAIM** (substantive pleading)

1. **Heading**.
2. **Detailed pleading** — facts · cause of action · evidence · quantum.
3. **Reliefs sought**.

**(D) AWARD ENFORCEMENT**

1. **DIFC-seated awards** — enforced via DIFC Court ratification + DIFC-Dubai Protocol for onshore execution + NYC for international enforcement.
2. **Mainland-seated awards** — enforced via Dubai Court of Appeal ratification + NYC for international enforcement.
3. **NYC enforcement** — UAE acceded 2006; awards enforceable in 168+ Contracting States.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Seat selection critical** — DIFC seat = DIFC Court supervisory jurisdiction + DIFC-Dubai Protocol enforcement; Mainland seat = Dubai Court supervisory jurisdiction.
- **DIAC Rules 2022** — post-merger updated rules; cover both DIFC and Mainland seats.
- **Federal Arbitration Law 6/2018** — modern arbitration framework (UNCITRAL Model Law influence) for Mainland seats.
- **DIFC Arbitration Law 1/2008** — for DIFC-seated arbitrations.
- **Public policy challenges** — UAE courts may refuse enforcement on public-policy grounds (Sharia · constitutional rights · sovereign immunity).
- **Currency** — typically USD or AED; foreign-currency awards enforceable.
- **Tribunal appointment** — sole arbitrator or three-arbitrator panel; DIAC default 1 arbitrator if dispute below AED 750k.
- **Expedited procedure** — DIAC Rules Art 32: expedited for disputes below USD 3m.
- **Emergency Arbitrator** — DIAC Rules Art 33: pre-tribunal urgent relief.
- **Confidentiality** — DIAC arbitrations confidential by default.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"An arbitration agreement is enforceable in accordance with its terms. The arbitral award is final and binding on the parties. Awards rendered in the State shall be enforceable through the competent court after ratification." (Federal Law No. 6 of 2018 on Arbitration + DIFC Arbitration Law No. 1 of 2008, synthesised.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
DIAC arbitration is the principal commercial dispute-resolution forum for international parties in Dubai. Solo practitioners advising contractually should: (a) draft clear arbitration clauses naming seat · language · tribunal composition · governing law; (b) consider DIFC seat for international parties (common-law procedure + English-language); (c) use NYC enforcement framework for global recoveries. The 2021 merger of DIFC-LCIA + EMAC + DIAC consolidated the Dubai arbitration landscape under DIAC Rules 2022.
