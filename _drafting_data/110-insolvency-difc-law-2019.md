## Currency Warning
DIFC Insolvency Law 2019 (replaced earlier 2009 Law). Three principal procedures: (a) **Rehabilitation** — court-supervised restructuring; (b) **Administration** — appointed administrator manages estate; (c) **Liquidation** — winding-up + distribution. Available for DIFC-registered companies + DIFC branches of foreign companies.

## Template Type
Application + Supporting Documents

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) WINDING-UP APPLICATION**

1. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · case number · year · In the Matter of `<Company>`.
2. **Title** — "Application for Winding-Up Order under DIFC Insolvency Law 2019".
3. **Applicant** — creditor / shareholder / Company itself / DFSA (regulated entities).
4. **Company particulars** — DIFC registration · paid-up capital · directors · DFSA license (if applicable).
5. **Grounds**:
   - **Unable to pay debts** (Art 14 Insolvency Law) — failure to comply with statutory demand · failure to satisfy judgment · cash-flow insolvency · balance-sheet insolvency.
   - **Just and equitable** — limited grounds.
6. **Sum due + supporting documents**.
7. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Order winding-up the company.
   - Appointment of Liquidator (named DFSA-registered insolvency practitioner OR Official Liquidator).
   - Costs.
8. **Advertisement** — DIFC Gazette + UAE newspaper.

**(B) ADMINISTRATION APPLICATION** (Insolvency Law Part 3)

1. **Application** by directors · shareholders · creditors.
2. **Affidavit**:
   - Company insolvent or likely to become.
   - Reasonable prospect of better outcome than liquidation.
   - Proposed Administrator (DFSA-registered insolvency practitioner).
3. **Effect of Administration**:
   - Moratorium on creditor actions (Art 50 Insolvency Law).
   - Administrator takes control; directors' powers suspended.
   - 4-month statutory period (extendable).

**(C) REHABILITATION** (Insolvency Law Part 2)

1. **Application** for court-supervised restructuring.
2. **Restructuring plan** — proposed treatment of creditors.
3. **Class voting** — creditor classes vote on plan.
4. **Court sanction** — DIFC Court approves plan if conditions met.

## Drafting Conventions
- **DFSA-registered insolvency practitioners** — only DFSA-licensed practitioners may act.
- **DIFC Gazette** advertisement mandatory for winding-up.
- **Cross-border insolvency** — UNCITRAL Model Law incorporated via DIFC Insolvency Regulations Part 9.
- **DIFC-Dubai Protocol** — DIFC insolvency orders coordinated with onshore proceedings where group structure.
- **Priority creditors** — DIFC Insolvency Law Art 102: secured · expenses · employees · preferred · ordinary unsecured · subordinated.
- **Voidable transactions** — uncommercial transactions · unfair preferences · within prescribed periods (Art 90-100 Insolvency Law).
- **Disqualification of directors** — DFSA may apply for director-disqualification orders for misconduct (Art 144).
- **Coordinated foreign insolvency** — recognition of foreign main + non-main proceedings under UNCITRAL Model Law.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A company shall be deemed unable to pay its debts if a creditor has obtained a final judgment or order against the company and the company has failed to satisfy the judgment within a reasonable period, or if it is established that the company is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, or that the value of its assets is less than the amount of its liabilities." (DIFC Insolvency Law No. 1 of 2019, Article 14, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
DIFC insolvency framework is sophisticated and DFSA-regulated. Solo practitioners advising creditors of DIFC-registered companies should consider (a) winding-up for clean recovery; (b) Administration where rehabilitation feasible; (c) Rehabilitation for going-concern preservation. Cross-border coordination with onshore Dubai mainland insolvency increasingly common; UNCITRAL Model Law recognition framework facilitates.
