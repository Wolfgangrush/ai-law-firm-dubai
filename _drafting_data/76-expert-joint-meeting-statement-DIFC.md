Court Rules (RDC Rule 31.6)

English (Primary)

## Currency Warning
Joint Expert Meetings under RDC 31.6 increasingly directed in complex DIFC Court matters. Conducted without-prejudice; produces joint statement narrowing issues. Court may direct joint meeting at Case Management Conference or interlocutory hearing.

## Template Type
Procedural Document / Joint Statement

## Structural Skeleton

**Pre-Meeting**

1. **Court direction** for joint meeting (RDC 31.6) — typically at CMC.
2. **Agreed list of questions** — narrow set of questions experts will address.
3. **Time + location** — typically without legal representatives; without-prejudice.
4. **Materials** — both experts' reports + key documents.

**Joint Meeting**

1. Conducted by experts; instructing solicitors typically not present.
2. Discussion of each question; agreed answers + disagreed answers identified.
3. Reasons for disagreements articulated.
4. Outcomes documented.

**JOINT STATEMENT**

1. **Heading** — DIFC Courts · case number · parties · expert names + qualifications · date.
2. **Title** — "Joint Statement of Experts Pursuant to Order of [date]".
3. **Conference particulars** — date · time · location · format.
4. **Questions addressed** (verbatim from court direction).
5. **Agreed matters**:
   - For each question: agreed answer + agreed reasons.
   - Binding for trial (subject to changes in evidence).
6. **Disagreed matters**:
   - For each disputed question: each expert's position + reasoning.
   - Scope of disagreement narrowed to essential point.
7. **Areas requiring further investigation** (where applicable).
8. **Joint recommendations**.
9. **Signatures** — both experts; date.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Without-prejudice** — communications in joint meeting confidential; cannot be used against either party at trial.
- **Experts only at meeting** — legal representatives excluded unless court orders otherwise.
- **Joint statement binding** at trial — experts who depart from joint statement face cross-examination on credibility.
- **Bullet-point format** preferred — concise; long discursive statements disfavoured.
- **Sequence of meetings** — multiple meetings may be directed at different stages.
- **Hot-tubbing follows** — at trial, experts may be cross-examined concurrently before bench based on joint statement.
- **Costs of failure to meet** — adverse costs orders against recalcitrant party.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court may direct experts to meet for the purpose of identifying matters on which their evidence will be in agreement and matters on which their evidence will not be in agreement, and producing a joint statement of those matters." (RDC Rule 31.6, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Joint Expert Meetings are mandatory in DIFC Court complex litigation. Solo practitioners should engage the expert pre-meeting to ensure they understand the without-prejudice nature + their overriding duty to the Court (joint statement is NOT a negotiated settlement). The joint statement substantially narrows trial issues + drives trial strategy.
